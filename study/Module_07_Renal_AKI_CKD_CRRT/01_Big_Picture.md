# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **The kidney is a pressure-driven filtration plant that fine-tunes the entire internal
> environment — so when it fails, K⁺, acid, volume, and BP all go wrong together.**

## What the kidney does — mnemonic "A WET BED"
| Letter | Job | Fails → |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | **A**cid–base (excrete H⁺, regenerate HCO₃⁻) | metabolic acidosis |
| **W** | **W**ater / volume balance | fluid overload, edema, HTN |
| **E** | **E**rythropoietin (EPO → red cells) | anemia |
| **T** | **T**oxin / waste removal (urea, Cr, drugs) | uremia, drug accumulation |
| **B** | **B**P control (RAAS / renin) | hypertension |
| **E** | **E**lectrolytes (esp. K⁺, Na⁺, Phos) | **hyperkalemia**, ↑Phos |
| **D** | vitamin **D** activation → Ca²⁺/bone | low Ca²⁺, bone disease |

## The nephron — 3 steps (engineering view)
```
1. FILTER  — glomerulus: pressure-driven ultrafiltration → GFR (throughput)
2. REABSORB — tubules: selectively recover water, Na⁺, glucose, HCO₃⁻ (keep the good)
3. SECRETE — tubules: actively dump K⁺, H⁺, drugs (excrete the waste)
```
- **GFR is flow/pressure-dependent.** Low MAP → low GFR → low urine (pre-renal). The kidney
  is the body's flow gauge — that's why **urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr** is a perfusion vital.
- **RAAS** = the BP/volume control loop: low flow → renin → angiotensin II (vasoconstrict)
  + aldosterone (retain Na⁺/water) → ↑BP. (Same loop ACE inhibitors block — Mod 4.)

## The 3 ways AKI happens (memorize this split)
| Type | Problem | Cause examples | BUN:Cr |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pre-renal** | not enough blood TO the kidney | shock, hypovolemia, HF (most common) | **>20:1** |
| **Intrinsic** | the kidney tissue is damaged | **ATN** from ischemia or nephrotoxins | ~10–15:1 |
| **Post-renal** | obstruction blocking outflow | stones, BPH, tumor, clot | varies |

## 5 anchors
1. **A WET BED** = the kidney's 7 jobs; AKI breaks all of them.
2. **Pre / intrinsic / post** — classify every AKI; it changes treatment.
3. **Pre-renal is the shock connection** — Module 01 ATN: low flow → tubular cells starve → die.
4. **Failed kidney → hyperkalemia + metabolic acidosis + fluid overload** = the lethal trio (AEIOU).
5. **CRRT = slow continuous dialysis** for the unstable ICU patient (Mod 2).
