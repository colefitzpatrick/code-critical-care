# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → which renal problem → priority action.**

### Case 1 — Septic patient, MAP 55, urine 10 mL over 2 h, Cr rising, BUN:Cr 28:1
- **Mechanism:** **pre-renal AKI** — hypoperfusion (Mod 01 spiral) → ↓GFR. BUN:Cr >20:1 confirms.
- **Priority:** fix perfusion — sepsis Hour-1 bundle, fluids, vasopressors to MAP ≥65. Catch it before ATN sets in. Monitor UOP hourly.

### Case 2 — Dialysis patient missed 2 sessions: K⁺ 6.9, peaked T waves, weak
- **Mechanism:** no renal K⁺ exit + no dialysis → **hyperkalemia** (Mod 1 Chain 4).
- **Priority:** cardiac monitor + 12-lead, notify, **stabilize (IV calcium) → shift (insulin+glucose, albuterol) → remove (urgent dialysis)**. This is an emergency.

### Case 3 — AKI patient now making 4 L urine/day, K⁺ 3.0, BP dropping
- **Mechanism:** **diuretic phase** of AKI — kidney dumping fluid; now hypovolemia + **hypokalemia**.
- **Priority:** flip the worry — replace fluids + K⁺ (never push), monitor closely. The danger reversed from the oliguric phase.

### Case 4 — Patient scheduled for CT with contrast, baseline Cr 2.1
- **Mechanism:** contrast = nephrotoxin → risk contrast-induced AKI in impaired kidney.
- **Priority:** flag it — verify hydration order, hold nephrotoxins, ensure provider aware of Cr; advocate for renal-protective protocol.

### Case 5 — CKD patient, fatigued, Hgb 8.5, Ca 7.8, Phos 6.5
- **Mechanism:** CKD complications — anemia (↓EPO) + bone–mineral disease (↓vit D → ↓Ca, ↑phos).
- **Priority:** anticipate EPO + iron, **phosphate binders WITH meals**, vit D/Ca management; teach diet (low phos/K), fluid limits, adherence.
