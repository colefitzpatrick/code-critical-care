<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. Acid-base, Water/volume, EPO, Toxin removal, BP (RAAS), Electrolytes, vit D activation.
- A2. Filter (glomerulus, pressure-driven GFR); Reabsorb (recover water/Na/glucose/HCO₃); Secrete (dump K⁺/H⁺/drugs).
- A3. Filtration is pressure-driven, so low MAP → low GFR; bedside gauge = **urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr**.
- A4. Low flow → renin → angiotensin II (vasoconstrict) + aldosterone (retain Na/water) → ↑BP/volume.

**B**
- B1. Pre-renal = ↓flow (shock/hypovolemia/HF); Intrinsic = tissue damage (ATN ischemic/toxic); Post-renal = obstruction (stones/BPH/tumor).
- B2. Pre-renal **>20:1**; intrinsic ~10–15:1.
- B3. Persistent low flow → tubular cells run out of ATP → die (ATN) → pre-renal becomes intrinsic.
- B4. Contrast dye, NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, vancomycin (also ACE in low-flow). Prevent: hydrate around contrast, monitor levels/Cr, avoid NSAIDs in at-risk.

**C**
- C1. Hyperkalemia + metabolic acidosis + fluid overload (+ uremia).
- C2. Acidosis → H⁺ into cells, K⁺ out → adds to hyperkalemia.
- C3. Oliguric = overload + ↑K⁺ danger; diuretic phase = ↓volume + **↓K⁺** danger — flip the worry.
- C4. **A**cidosis, **E**lectrolytes (hyperK), **I**ntoxication, **O**verload, **U**remia.

**D**
- D1. Diabetes (#1) and hypertension (#2); staged by **GFR** (Stage 5/ESRD <15 → dialysis).
- D2. Anemia (↓EPO), bone–mineral disease (↓vit D/↓Ca/↑phos/↑PTH), hyperK, metabolic acidosis, fluid overload/HTN, uremia.
- D3. HD = intermittent, needs stable BP; CRRT = continuous, for hemodynamically unstable ICU patients.
- D4. Protect the arm (no BP cuff, no IV, no venipuncture); check patency by feeling the **thrill** + hearing the **bruit**.
</details>
