## Anatomy / function
- **Nephron** — the kidney's filtering unit (~1 million per kidney).
- **Glomerulus** — the filter; pressure-driven ultrafiltration.
- **GFR (glomerular filtration rate)** — how much blood the kidney filters per minute; flow/pressure-dependent. Stages CKD.
- **Reabsorption** — tubules recovering water/Na⁺/glucose/HCO₃⁻ back into blood.
- **Secretion** — tubules actively dumping K⁺, H⁺, drugs into urine.
- **RAAS** — renin–angiotensin–aldosterone system; the BP/volume control loop (renin → angiotensin II vasoconstriction + aldosterone Na⁺/water retention).
- **EPO (erythropoietin)** — kidney hormone that tells marrow to make red cells; low in CKD → anemia.
- **A WET BED** — kidney jobs: Acid-base, Water/volume, EPO, Toxins, BP, Electrolytes, vit D.

## AKI
- **AKI (acute kidney injury)** — sudden drop in kidney function (↑Cr, ↓urine).
- **Pre-renal** — low blood flow TO the kidney (shock, hypovolemia, HF). Most common.
- **Intrinsic / intra-renal** — kidney tissue damage; usually **ATN**.
- **ATN (acute tubular necrosis)** — tubular cell death from ischemia or nephrotoxins.
- **Post-renal** — obstruction blocking urine OUT (stones, BPH, tumor, clot).
- **BUN:Cr ratio** — >20:1 suggests pre-renal (kidney avidly reabsorbing).
- **Nephrotoxins** — agents that damage the kidney: contrast dye, NSAIDs, aminoglycosides, vancomycin, (ACE in low-flow states).
- **AKI phases** — Onset → Oliguric (overload, ↑K⁺, acidosis) → Diuretic (↓volume, ↓K⁺) → Recovery.
- **Oliguria** — low urine (<0.5 mL/kg/hr); **anuria** — essentially none.

## CKD & complications
- **CKD (chronic kidney disease)** — progressive, irreversible nephron loss; staged by GFR (Stage 5 / ESRD <15 → dialysis). Top causes: diabetes, hypertension.
- **Uremia** — toxic waste buildup (confusion, pericarditis, bleeding, nausea).
- **CKD-mineral bone disease** — ↓vit D → ↓Ca²⁺ → ↑PTH + ↑phosphate → bone breakdown.
- **Phosphate binder** — drug taken WITH meals to stop phosphate absorption.
- **AEIOU** — dialysis indications: Acidosis, Electrolytes (hyperK), Intoxication, Overload, Uremia.

## Dialysis & access
- **Hemodialysis (HD)** — intermittent (3–4 h, ~3×/wk); needs stable BP.
- **CRRT (continuous renal replacement therapy)** — slow 24/7 dialysis for hemodynamically unstable ICU patients.
- **Peritoneal dialysis** — uses the peritoneal membrane; often home-based.
- **AV fistula / graft** — surgical vein–artery connection for dialysis access. Protect the arm: no BP, no IV, no venipuncture.
- **Thrill** — the palpable buzz over a working fistula. **Bruit** — the audible whoosh. No thrill/bruit = clotted = emergency.
