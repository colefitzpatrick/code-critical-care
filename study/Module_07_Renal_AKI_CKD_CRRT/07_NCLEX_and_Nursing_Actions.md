# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Renal is heavily tested because it links to fluids, electrolytes, acid–base, cardiac, and
drugs. Do this twice.

## A. Priority rule
**ABC + the hyperkalemia/fluid-overload killers come first.**
- A dialysis patient with peaked T waves / K⁺ >6 = top priority (cardiac).
- Fluid overload with crackles + low SpO₂ = airway/breathing priority.
- New oliguria in a sick patient = catch pre-renal early.

## B. Assessment priorities
- **Daily weight (same scale/time)** + **strict I&O** + **urine output ≥0.5 mL/kg/hr** — the renal vital signs.
- Lung sounds, edema, JVD (overload) · BP · mental status (uremia/electrolytes).
- **K⁺ + ECG** every time renal function is off.
- Dialysis access: **thrill (palpate) + bruit (auscultate)** each shift.

## C. Drug → why → monitor
| Drug/class | Use | Watch | Trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loop diuretic (furosemide) | offload fluid (if kidney still responds) | ↓K⁺, ototoxicity, volume/BP | won't work in anuric ESRD |
| Phosphate binders (sevelamer, Ca acetate) | lower phosphate in CKD | **give WITH meals** | given between meals = useless |
| EPO (epoetin) + iron | CKD anemia | BP↑, thrombosis; check Hgb | over-correcting Hgb = clots |
| Active vitamin D / Ca | CKD bone disease | Ca level | with high phos → calcification |
| Sodium polystyrene / patiromer | remove K⁺ (GI) | slow onset; bowel | not for acute lethal hyperK alone |
| IV calcium / insulin+glucose / albuterol | acute hyperK | ECG, glucose | calcium stabilizes, doesn't lower K⁺ |
| **Hold:** NSAIDs, ACE/ARB (in AKI), nephrotoxic abx | prevent further injury | renal function | giving them worsens AKI |

## D. Labs you act on
- **K⁺** (the killer) · **HCO₃⁻** (acidosis) · **BUN / Cr / GFR** (function) · **BUN:Cr ratio** (>20:1 pre-renal)
- **Phos ↑ / Ca ↓** (CKD bone disease) · **Hgb ↓** (anemia) · daily weight as a "lab"

## E. Patient teaching (NCLEX "shows understanding")
- **Renal diet:** limit **sodium, potassium, phosphorus**, and fluid; protein per stage/dialysis status.
- **Fluid restriction:** measure intake, suck ice chips, track daily weight, report >2–3 lb/day.
- **Phosphate binders:** take **with every meal**.
- **Fistula care:** protect the arm (no BP/IV/blood draws), check the buzz daily, keep clean, report no thrill/bleeding/numbness.
- **Avoid OTC NSAIDs** and contrast without telling providers about kidney disease.
- **Adherence to dialysis** schedule — missing a session = dangerous K⁺ rise.

## F. Safety & delegation
- **RN:** dialysis-access assessment, hyperK management, AKI assessment, teaching, titrating.
- **UAP can:** daily weights/I&O on stable patients, vitals (report), ambulate stable — **but never take BP or draw blood from a fistula arm; flag the arm.**
- Post-contrast / nephrotoxic orders: verify hydration + baseline Cr first.

## G. NCLEX test-taking on renal
- "Which arm for BP?" → **never** the fistula arm.
- Hyperkalemia order of treatment: **calcium (stabilize) → insulin/glucose + albuterol (shift) → dialysis/binders (remove).**
- Phosphate binder timing = **with meals** (classic "needs more teaching" item if they say between meals).
- Oliguric phase = worry overload/↑K; diuretic phase = worry hypovolemia/↓K (they love this flip).
- Pre-renal → give fluids; intrinsic/overloaded → fluids harm. Match action to AKI type.
