# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → which gland/hormone/direction → priority action.**

### Case 1 — Type 1 diabetic, glucose 480, Kussmaul breathing, fruity breath, K⁺ 5.4
- **Mechanism:** **DKA** — no insulin → ketoacids → high-anion-gap acidosis (Mod 2).
- **Priority:** isotonic fluids, insulin infusion, **watch K⁺** (it will crash with insulin), hourly glucose/lytes, find precipitant. Don't chase the bicarb — treat the cause.

### Case 2 — Diabetic, glucose 38, diaphoretic, confused, can't safely swallow
- **Mechanism:** **hypoglycemia** — brain starving.
- **Priority:** treat FAST — no safe swallow/IV → **glucagon IM**; if IV → **D50**; recheck in 15 min. This beats almost everything on the unit.

### Case 3 — Post-op thyroidectomy, fever 104, HR 160, agitated
- **Mechanism:** **thyroid storm**.
- **Priority:** cool, **beta-blocker** for rate, antithyroid meds, quiet environment, monitor; emergency.

### Case 4 — Patient who stopped prednisone abruptly: BP 78/40, glucose 50, Na⁺ 128, K⁺ 5.8
- **Mechanism:** **adrenal crisis** (Addisonian) — cortisol collapse.
- **Priority:** **IV hydrocortisone + isotonic fluids**, treat hypoglycemia, monitor. Teach never to stop steroids abruptly.

### Case 5 — Post-craniotomy: Na⁺ 122, low urine output, concentrated urine
- **Mechanism:** **SIADH** (too much ADH → water retention → dilutional hyponatremia).
- **Priority:** fluid restriction, careful/slow Na⁺ correction, seizure precautions, neuro checks. (Contrast: DI = high Na⁺ + huge dilute urine.)

## Transferable move
Cue → gland + hormone + direction (too much/little) → check glucose/Na⁺/K⁺ → act on the
emergency form if present.
