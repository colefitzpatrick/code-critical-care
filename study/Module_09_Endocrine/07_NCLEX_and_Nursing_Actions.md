# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Endocrine is dense with "which is it + what do you do" items. Do twice.

## A. Priority rule
**Treat the crisis + the brain's fuel first.** Hypoglycemia, adrenal crisis, thyroid storm, and myxedema coma are time-critical. Hypoglycemia usually beats hyperglycemia for urgency.

## B. Glucose emergencies — fast reference
| | DKA | HHS | Hypoglycemia |
|---|---|---|---|
| Type | 1 | 2 | either |
| Glucose | 300–600 | >600 | <70 |
| Ketones/acidosis | yes (high-AG) | no | no |
| First action | fluids → insulin, watch K⁺ | fluids (lots) → insulin, watch K⁺ | 15 g carbs / glucagon / D50 |

## C. Drugs → why → watch
| Drug | Use | Watch |
|---|---|---|
| Insulin (rapid/short/long) | lower glucose | **hypoglycemia + hypokalemia** (shifts K⁺ in) |
| Glucagon / D50 | hypoglycemia rescue | recheck glucose |
| Levothyroxine | hypothyroid replacement | take on empty stomach AM; cardiac (start low in elderly) |
| Beta-blocker (storm) | control tachy in hyperthyroid | HR/BP |
| Methimazole/PTU | antithyroid (hyper) | agranulocytosis (report sore throat/fever) |
| **Hydrocortisone** | adrenal insufficiency/crisis | glucose, Na⁺/K⁺, BP; never stop abruptly |
| Desmopressin (DDAVP) | DI | Na⁺, fluid balance |

## D. Labs you act on
- **Glucose** (and ketones, anion gap in DKA) · **K⁺** (insulin shifts it) · **Na⁺** (SIADH/DI)
- **TSH** (high = hypothyroid; low = hyperthyroid) · cortisol · A1c (≥6.5% diabetic)

## E. Teaching (NCLEX "shows understanding")
- **Diabetes:** rotate insulin sites, don't skip insulin when sick (sick-day rules), recognize/treat hypoglycemia (rule of 15), foot care, A1c goal.
- **Steroids:** **never stop abruptly**, take with food, may raise glucose/BP, medical-alert ID, stress-dosing.
- **Levothyroxine:** lifelong, same time daily on empty stomach, don't switch brands casually.
- **Addison's:** medical-alert ID, carry emergency hydrocortisone, increase dose with stress/illness.

## F. Safety & delegation
- **RN:** insulin/drips, crisis management, teaching, first assessment.
- **UAP can:** routine glucometer checks (per policy, report), vitals, ADLs — RN interprets/acts.
- Insulin is a **high-alert med** — double-check, never abbreviate "units" as "U."

## G. NCLEX test-taking
- Hypoglycemia → treat **first/fast** (it's the immediate threat).
- DKA/HHS → fluids before/with insulin; **anticipate K⁺ dropping** with insulin.
- High TSH = hypothyroid (the pituitary is pushing a lazy gland).
- "Stopped steroids" + hypotension/hypoglycemia = **adrenal crisis** → hydrocortisone.
- SIADH = low Na⁺ (restrict fluid); DI = high Na⁺ (replace water/DDAVP); correct **slowly**.
