## Diabetes
- **Insulin** — hormone that moves glucose into cells (and K⁺ in).
- **Type 1 DM** — autoimmune, no insulin made.
- **Type 2 DM** — insulin resistance ± relative deficiency.
- **DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis)** — Type 1: no insulin → ketoacids → high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis (Mod 2).
- **HHS (hyperosmolar hyperglycemic state)** — Type 2: glucose very high (>600), hyperosmolar, dehydration, no significant ketones/acidosis.
- **Hypoglycemia** — glucose <70; brain-starving emergency; "rule of 15."
- **Kussmaul breathing** — deep/fast breathing blowing off CO₂ in DKA.
- **A1c** — 3-month average glucose; ≥6.5% = diabetic.
- **Glucagon** — hormone/drug that raises glucose (hypoglycemia rescue without IV).

## Thyroid
- **TSH** — pituitary hormone that stimulates the thyroid; high TSH = hypothyroid.
- **Hyperthyroidism / Graves** — too much thyroid hormone (fast metabolism).
- **Thyroid storm** — life-threatening hyperthyroid crisis (fever, tachy, agitation).
- **Hypothyroidism / Hashimoto** — too little thyroid hormone (slow).
- **Myxedema coma** — extreme hypothyroid crisis (hypothermia, ↓LOC, bradycardia).
- **Levothyroxine** — synthetic thyroid hormone replacement.
- **Methimazole / PTU** — antithyroid drugs (watch agranulocytosis).

## Adrenal
- **Cortisol** — stress hormone (raises glucose/BP, suppresses immunity).
- **Aldosterone** — retains Na⁺/water, excretes K⁺.
- **Cushing's syndrome** — too much cortisol (moon face, ↑glucose, ↑BP, ↓K⁺).
- **Addison's disease** — adrenal insufficiency (↓BP, ↓glucose, ↓Na⁺, ↑K⁺, hyperpigmentation).
- **Adrenal crisis** — acute cortisol collapse (hypotension, hypoglycemia, hyperK) — IV hydrocortisone.

## Pituitary / parathyroid
- **ADH (antidiuretic hormone)** — holds water.
- **SIADH** — too much ADH → water retention → low Na⁺.
- **DI (diabetes insipidus)** — too little ADH → water loss → high Na⁺, huge dilute urine.
- **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** — synthetic ADH; treats DI.
- **PTH (parathyroid hormone)** — raises calcium (Mod 2).
