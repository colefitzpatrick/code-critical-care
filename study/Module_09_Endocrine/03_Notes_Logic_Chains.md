# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — Diabetes & insulin
**Insulin** = the key that moves glucose INTO cells (and K⁺ in — Mod 1/2). **Type 1** = no insulin (autoimmune); **Type 2** = insulin resistance.
**→ nurse:** match insulin type to need (rapid/short for meals & correction; long/basal for background); monitor glucose; teach carb awareness, sick-day rules, hypoglycemia treatment.

## Chain 2 — DKA vs HHS (know the split cold)
No insulin → cells starve → liver burns fat → **ketoacids** → **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis** (Mod 2 worked example) = **DKA** (Type 1; glucose ~300–600; Kussmaul; fruity breath).
**HHS** (Type 2): enough insulin to block ketones but not hyperglycemia → glucose **>600**, profound dehydration, **hyperosmolar**, no significant acidosis, ↓LOC.
**→ nurse (both):** IV **isotonic fluids** first, **insulin** infusion, and **watch K⁺ obsessively** — it crashes as insulin drives K⁺ into cells; treat precipitant (infection/missed insulin); hourly glucose/lytes.

## Chain 3 — Hypoglycemia (the fast emergency)
Glucose <70 → brain starves (no fuel reserve, Mod 8) → shaky, sweaty, confused → seizure/coma
**→ nurse:** **treat FAST** — awake: 15 g fast carbs, recheck in 15 min (rule of 15); not awake/no IV: **glucagon**; IV access: **D50**. Always recheck. *Hypoglycemia kills faster than hyperglycemia.*

## Chain 4 — Thyroid: hyper vs hypo (+ their crises)
**Hyperthyroid** (Graves) = everything fast: weight loss, tachy, heat intolerance, anxiety → **thyroid storm** = life-threatening: high fever, tachyarrhythmia, agitation.
**Hypothyroid** (Hashimoto) = everything slow: fatigue, cold, weight gain, bradycardia → **myxedema coma** = hypothermia, ↓LOC, bradycardia, hypoventilation.
**→ nurse:** storm → cooling, **beta-blocker** (rate), antithyroid drugs, quiet environment; myxedema → **thyroid hormone** replacement, warm gently, airway/ventilation support. **TSH** is the screening test (high TSH = hypothyroid).

## Chain 5 — Adrenal: Cushing's vs Addison's (+ crisis)
**Cortisol** = stress hormone (glucose↑, BP support, immune suppression).
**Cushing's** (too much) = moon face, central obesity, thin skin, high glucose, HTN, low K⁺.
**Addison's** (too little) = fatigue, **hypotension**, hypoglycemia, **low Na⁺ / high K⁺**, hyperpigmentation.
**→ Adrenal crisis** (acute cortisol collapse — often from abruptly stopping steroids or stress): **profound hypotension + hypoglycemia + hyperK** → **IV hydrocortisone + isotonic fluids**, treat the stressor.
**→ nurse:** **never stop steroids abruptly**; stress-dose steroids during illness/surgery; teach taper + medical-alert ID.

## Chain 6 — Posterior pituitary: SIADH vs DI (Mod 7/8 link)
**ADH** holds water. **SIADH** (too much ADH) → retain water → **hyponatremia** (concentrated urine, low output) → cerebral edema risk.
**DI** (too little ADH) → dump water → **hypernatremia** (huge dilute urine, thirst).
**→ nurse:** SIADH → fluid restriction, careful Na⁺ correction; DI → replace fluids + **desmopressin (DDAVP)**; both → monitor Na⁺/osmolality/UOP and **correct slowly** (Mod 2 brain-water rule).

## Chain 7 — Steroids & the cross-system links
Long-term steroids → mimic Cushing's (high glucose, immune suppression, thin skin, osteoporosis) AND suppress the adrenal axis (so stopping abruptly → crisis).
**→ insulin/glucose ↔ K⁺** (Mod 1/2): insulin shifts K⁺ in (hypoK in treatment; hyperK if no insulin). **DKA ↔ acid-base** (Mod 2). **SIADH/DI ↔ Na⁺/brain** (Mod 8).
**→ nurse:** any endocrine patient = check **glucose, Na⁺, K⁺** and think about feedback (what's the gland, the hormone, the direction?).

---
### Compression (20 s)
> Endocrine = feedback loops, too much/too little hormone. DKA (Type 1, ketones, acidosis) vs HHS (Type 2, hyperosmolar, no ketones) vs hypoglycemia (treat fast). Thyroid storm/myxedema, Cushing's/Addison's (+crisis), SIADH (↓Na)/DI (↑Na). Never stop steroids abruptly; watch K⁺ in DKA treatment.
