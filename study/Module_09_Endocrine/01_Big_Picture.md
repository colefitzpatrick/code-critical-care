# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **Every endocrine disease is a feedback loop with too much or too little hormone — name the gland, the hormone, and the direction, and the signs/treatment fall out.**

## The control-loop frame (Mod 01 Ch6)
Gland → hormone → target effect → **negative feedback** turns the gland down.
- **Too much hormone** = the "hyper" disease (or a tumor/exogenous source).
- **Too little hormone** = the "hypo" disease (gland failure or destruction).
- Many are diagnosed by the **stimulating hormone**: e.g., high TSH = the pituitary yelling at a lazy (hypo) thyroid.

## The 5 systems in this module
| Axis | Hormone | Too much | Too little |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pancreas | Insulin | hypoglycemia (exogenous) | **Diabetes / DKA / HHS** |
| Thyroid | T3/T4 | **Hyperthyroid → thyroid storm** | **Hypothyroid → myxedema coma** |
| Adrenal cortex | Cortisol (±aldosterone) | **Cushing's** | **Addison's → adrenal crisis** |
| Posterior pituitary | ADH | **SIADH** (retain water → ↓Na) | **DI** (lose water → ↑Na) |
| Parathyroid | PTH | hyperCa²⁺ | hypoCa²⁺ (Mod 2) |

## Engineering translation
- Insulin = the key that lets glucose **into** cells (and drives K⁺ in — Mod 1/2).
- DKA = no insulin → cells starve → liver makes ketoacids → **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis** (Mod 2 worked example).
- Negative feedback set points: a gland tumor ignores the brake (runs hot); gland destruction = no output despite the brain screaming (high stimulating hormone).

## 5 anchors
1. Name **gland + hormone + direction** → the disease decodes itself.
2. **DKA (Type 1, ketones, acidosis)** vs **HHS (Type 2, huge glucose, hyperosmolar, no ketones)** vs **hypoglycemia (treat FAST)**.
3. **Thyroid storm** and **myxedema coma** and **adrenal crisis** are the can't-miss emergencies.
4. **SIADH = low Na⁺ / DI = high Na⁺** (Mod 7/8) — correct slowly.
5. **Never stop steroids abruptly** → can trigger adrenal crisis.
