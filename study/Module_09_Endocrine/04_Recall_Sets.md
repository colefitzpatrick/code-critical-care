<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. Moves glucose into cells (and drives K⁺ into cells).
- A2. DKA: Type 1, ketones+, glucose ~300–600, high-anion-gap acidosis. HHS: Type 2, no ketones, glucose >600, hyperosmolar, minimal acidosis.
- A3. Isotonic fluids → insulin infusion; watch **K⁺** (crashes as insulin shifts it into cells); treat precipitant.
- A4. <70; **rule of 15** (15 g carbs, recheck in 15 min); not awake → glucagon (no IV) or D50 (IV).

**B**
- B1. Hyper: weight loss, tachy, heat intolerance (storm). Hypo: fatigue, cold, bradycardia, weight gain (myxedema coma).
- B2. Storm = fever/tachy/agitation → cool, beta-blocker, antithyroid. Myxedema = hypothermia/↓LOC/bradycardia → thyroid hormone, warm, support airway.
- B3. Cushing's: ↑glucose, ↑BP, ↓K⁺, ↑Na⁺. Addison's: ↓glucose, ↓BP, ↑K⁺, ↓Na⁺.
- B4. Triggers: abruptly stopping steroids, major stress/illness. Drug: **IV hydrocortisone** + isotonic fluids.

**C**
- C1. SIADH: ↑ADH → ↓Na⁺, low/concentrated urine. DI: ↓ADH → ↑Na⁺, high/dilute urine.
- C2. Abrupt stop → adrenal crisis (suppressed axis can't make cortisol).
- C3. Underactive (hypothyroid) — pituitary is pushing a lazy gland.
</details>
