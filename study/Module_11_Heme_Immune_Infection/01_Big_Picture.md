# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **Blood carries O₂ (red cells), defends (white cells/immune system), and seals leaks (platelets + clotting cascade) — and infection is when that defense system fights, sometimes turning on the host (sepsis/DIC).**

## The 3 jobs of blood
| Component | Job | Fails → |
|---|---|---|
| **Red cells (Hgb)** | carry O₂ | **anemia** (Mod 1 anemic hypoxia) |
| **White cells / immune** | defense | infection; **neutropenia** = no defense |
| **Platelets + clotting factors** | seal leaks | bleeding (low) or clotting (excess) |

## Coagulation (link Mod 01 Ch7 — positive feedback)
Injury → platelets plug → **clotting cascade** (each factor activates the next, amplifying) → fibrin clot → then regulated brakes stop it.
- Measured by **PT/INR** (extrinsic/warfarin) and **aPTT** (intrinsic/heparin).
- **DIC** = the cascade goes systemic and runs away → consumes all clotting factors/platelets → **clots everywhere AND bleeds everywhere** at once (clotting then hemorrhage).

## Immunity (two layers)
- **Innate** — fast, nonspecific (skin, neutrophils, inflammation, fever).
- **Adaptive** — slower, specific (lymphocytes, antibodies, memory).
- **Neutropenia** = too few neutrophils → can't fight bacteria → **neutropenic precautions** (the patient is defenseless; protect them).

## Sepsis (the deep dive — Mod 01)
Infection → **dysregulated** immune response → systemic vasodilation + capillary leak + microthrombi + mitochondrial dysfunction → **septic shock** (distributive). Trajectory: **infection → sepsis (organ dysfunction) → septic shock (vasopressor-dependent + lactate up)**.

## 5 anchors
1. Blood = **carry O₂ / defend / clot.**
2. **Transfusion reactions** are the can't-miss safety topic — stop the transfusion first.
3. **Coagulation = positive-feedback cascade; DIC = it running away** (clot then bleed).
4. **Neutropenia = defenseless** → protect the patient (neutropenic precautions).
5. **Sepsis Hour-1 bundle: L-C-A-F-V** (Mod 01); MAP ≥65.
