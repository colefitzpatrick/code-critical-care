# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → mechanism → priority action.**

### Case 1 — 10 min into PRBC transfusion: fever, flank pain, dark urine, BP dropping
- **Mechanism:** **acute hemolytic reaction** (ABO mismatch) — deadly.
- **Priority:** **STOP the transfusion immediately**, NS to keep line open, assess/vitals, notify provider + blood bank, send blood/urine per protocol, support BP. Stopping is the first action.

### Case 2 — Transfusion patient: sudden dyspnea, crackles, HTN, JVD
- **Mechanism:** **TACO** (transfusion-associated circulatory overload).
- **Priority:** stop/slow transfusion, sit upright, O₂, notify, anticipate diuretic. (Different from TRALI, which is hypoxia without overload signs.)

### Case 3 — Septic patient: oozing from IV sites + gums, ↓platelets, ↑PT/aPTT, ↑D-dimer
- **Mechanism:** **DIC** secondary to sepsis (Mod 01 Ch7 runaway).
- **Priority:** treat the **underlying sepsis** (Hour-1 bundle), replace products (platelets/FFP/cryo), monitor clot + bleed, escalate to ICU.

### Case 4 — Chemo patient, ANC very low, temp 38.5
- **Mechanism:** **neutropenic fever** — defenseless host, emergency.
- **Priority:** blood cultures + **broad-spectrum antibiotics promptly**, neutropenic precautions, monitor for sepsis; don't wait for other signs.

### Case 5 — Pneumonia patient: BP 84/48, HR 118, lactate 4.5, MAP 56 after fluids
- **Mechanism:** **septic shock** (distributive).
- **Priority:** Hour-1 bundle — cultures, antibiotics, 30 mL/kg fluids done, now **norepinephrine to MAP ≥65**, trend lactate, source control, ICU.

## Transferable move
Cue → red cell / immune / clotting problem? → for transfusions STOP first; for sepsis run
the bundle; for DIC treat the cause. The failures travel together (sepsis → ARDS/AKI/DIC).
