# _GLOSSARY — Module 11 (plain English)

## Red cells / anemia
- **Hemoglobin (Hgb)** — O₂-carrying protein in red cells.
- **Hematocrit (Hct)** — % of blood that is red cells.
- **Anemia** — low Hgb → less O₂ delivery (Mod 1 anemic hypoxia).
- **Iron-deficiency anemia** — low iron (blood loss/diet); most common.
- **Pernicious anemia** — B12 deficiency from lack of intrinsic factor.

## Transfusion
- **PRBC** — packed red blood cells (for anemia/blood loss).
- **FFP** — fresh frozen plasma (clotting factors).
- **Cryoprecipitate** — concentrated fibrinogen/factors.
- **Acute hemolytic reaction** — ABO mismatch; immune destruction of donor cells; deadly (fever, flank pain, dark urine, shock).
- **Febrile reaction** — fever/chills; most common, usually benign.
- **TACO** — transfusion-associated circulatory overload (volume overload).
- **TRALI** — transfusion-related acute lung injury (acute hypoxia).

## Coagulation
- **Platelets** — cell fragments that form the first plug.
- **Clotting cascade** — amplifying chain of clotting factors → fibrin (Mod 1 positive feedback).
- **PT/INR** — tracks the extrinsic pathway / warfarin.
- **aPTT** — tracks the intrinsic pathway / heparin.
- **Fibrinogen** — clotting protein; low in DIC.
- **D-dimer** — clot-breakdown marker; high in DIC/PE.
- **DIC (disseminated intravascular coagulation)** — runaway clotting that consumes factors → then bleeding; secondary to sepsis/OB/trauma/cancer.
- **HIT (heparin-induced thrombocytopenia)** — immune reaction to heparin → low platelets + paradoxical clotting; stop heparin.
- **Protamine** — reverses heparin. **Vitamin K / FFP** — reverses warfarin.

## Immune / infection
- **Innate immunity** — fast, nonspecific (neutrophils, inflammation, fever).
- **Adaptive immunity** — specific, memory (lymphocytes, antibodies).
- **Neutrophils / ANC** — front-line bacteria fighters / absolute neutrophil count.
- **Neutropenia** — low neutrophils → defenseless against infection.
- **Neutropenic precautions** — protect the patient (hygiene, private room, no sick visitors/raw foods).
- **Neutropenic fever** — fever in a neutropenic patient = emergency (cultures + antibiotics).
- **SIRS** — systemic inflammatory response (2+ of: T<36/>38, HR>90, RR>20, WBC<4/>12).
- **Sepsis** — life-threatening organ dysfunction from a dysregulated response to infection.
- **Septic shock** — sepsis + circulatory collapse (vasopressors + lactate >2).
- **Hour-1 bundle (L-C-A-F-V)** — Lactate, Cultures, Antibiotics, Fluids 30 mL/kg, Vasopressors.
