# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — Anemia (low O₂ carrying)
Low **Hgb** → less O₂ delivered (Mod 01 anemic hypoxia) → fatigue, pallor, tachycardia, dyspnea
**→ types:** iron-deficiency (blood loss/diet), B12/folate (macrocytic), anemia of chronic disease, acute blood loss
**→ nurse:** find the cause (esp. occult GI bleed), iron/B12 as indicated, transfuse if severe/symptomatic; monitor Hgb/Hct trend.

## Chain 2 — Transfusion safety (the can't-miss)
Donor blood must match (ABO/Rh). Errors = immune attack.
**→ Reactions:** **febrile** (common, fever/chills) · **allergic** (hives→anaphylaxis) · **acute hemolytic** (ABO mismatch — fever, flank/back pain, dark urine, shock — **deadly**) · **TACO** (circulatory overload — dyspnea, HTN, crackles) · **TRALI** (acute lung injury — hypoxia).
**→ nurse:** verify with 2 RNs + patient ID; stay first 15 min (most reactions early); vitals baseline + during. **ANY reaction → STOP the transfusion, keep the line open with saline, assess, notify** — stopping comes first.

## Chain 3 — Coagulation & monitoring
Injury → platelet plug → **clotting cascade** (amplifying, Mod 01 Ch7) → fibrin → regulated stop
**→** **PT/INR** tracks warfarin; **aPTT** tracks heparin; platelets must be adequate
**→ nurse:** anticoagulant teaching/bleeding precautions; know reversals — **vitamin K/FFP** for warfarin, **protamine** for heparin; hold for procedures.

## Chain 4 — DIC (the runaway — Mod 01 Ch7)
A trigger (sepsis #1, OB emergencies, trauma, cancer) → systemic clotting cascade activation → **microclots everywhere** → consumes all platelets + clotting factors → then **uncontrolled bleeding**
**→** labs: ↓platelets, ↑PT/aPTT, ↓fibrinogen, **↑D-dimer**
**→ nurse:** **treat the underlying cause** (it's secondary to something), support with blood products (platelets/FFP/cryo), monitor for both clotting AND bleeding; this is a death-spiral — escalate.

## Chain 5 — Immunity & neutropenia
Innate (fast/nonspecific) + adaptive (specific/memory). **Neutrophils** are the front-line bacteria fighters.
**→ Neutropenia** (chemo, marrow failure) → **defenseless** → even minor infection can kill; **fever in a neutropenic patient = emergency** (neutropenic fever → blood cultures + antibiotics fast)
**→ nurse:** **neutropenic precautions** — protect the patient: strict hand hygiene, private room, no sick visitors, no fresh flowers/raw produce (per policy), monitor for subtle infection signs (they may not mount a normal response).

## Chain 6 — Sepsis deep dive (Mod 01 integrated)
**Infection → sepsis** (dysregulated response causing organ dysfunction) **→ septic shock** (vasodilation + capillary leak + microthrombi + mitochondrial dysfunction → needs vasopressors + lactate up despite fluids)
**→ nurse:** recognize early (SIRS + source); run the **Hour-1 bundle (L-C-A-F-V)** — **L**actate, **C**ultures before antibiotics, **A**ntibiotics early, **F**luids 30 mL/kg, **V**asopressors (norepinephrine) to **MAP ≥65**; source control; trend lactate. *Time is tissue.*

## Chain 7 — The cross-module web
**→** Sepsis is the #1 cause of **ARDS** (Mod 6), **AKI** (Mod 7), and **DIC** (this module) — it cascades across organs.
**→** Anemia + poor perfusion = combined hypoxia (Mod 1 types). Transfusion overload = **TACO** → pulmonary edema (Mod 4/6). Heparin → **HIT** (immune-mediated low platelets + paradoxical clotting).
**→ nurse:** in any septic patient, watch lungs (ARDS), kidneys (UOP/AKI), and coag (DIC) — the failures travel together.

---
### Compression (20 s)
> Blood carries O₂ / defends / clots. Transfusion reaction → STOP it first (hemolytic = deadly). Coagulation = amplifying cascade; DIC = it running away (clot then bleed; treat the cause). Neutropenia = defenseless → protect + fever is an emergency. Sepsis: infection→sepsis→septic shock; Hour-1 L-C-A-F-V, MAP ≥65.
