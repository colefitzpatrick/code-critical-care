<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. Fatigue, pallor, tachycardia, dyspnea (less O₂ delivery). Types: iron-deficiency, B12/folate, chronic disease, acute blood loss.
- A2. Febrile, allergic, acute hemolytic (ABO mismatch — **deadly**), TACO (overload), TRALI (lung injury).
- A3. **STOP the transfusion**, keep line open with NS, assess, notify (then follow protocol).
- A4. Verify with 2 RNs + patient ID, baseline vitals, stay first 15 min, monitor vitals during.

**B**
- B1. PT/INR → warfarin (reverse: vitamin K/FFP); aPTT → heparin (reverse: protamine).
- B2. Cascade runs away → microclots consume platelets/factors → then bleeding. Labs: ↓platelets, ↑PT/aPTT, ↓fibrinogen, ↑D-dimer.
- B3. Treat the underlying cause; support with blood products; monitor clot + bleed.

**C**
- C1. No neutrophils = defenseless. Precautions: strict hand hygiene, private room, no sick visitors/fresh flowers/raw produce (per policy), monitor subtle signs.
- C2. They can't mount defense → infection can be lethal fast; **blood cultures + antibiotics promptly**.
- C3. Infection → organ dysfunction (sepsis) → vasopressor-dependent + lactate (septic shock). Hour-1: Lactate, Cultures, Antibiotics, Fluids 30 mL/kg, Vasopressors → MAP ≥65.
</details>
