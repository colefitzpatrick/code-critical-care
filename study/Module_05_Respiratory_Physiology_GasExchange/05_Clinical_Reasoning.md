# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → mechanism → priority action (Airway/Breathing first).**

### Case 1 — ARDS: SpO₂ 84% on 100% O₂, bilateral infiltrates
- **Mechanism:** widespread **shunt** — O₂ can't reach bypassed alveoli.
- **Priority:** escalate now — this won't fix with more FiO₂; anticipate PEEP/mechanical
  ventilation, positioning (prone per protocol), treat cause. Don't just sit on high O₂.

### Case 2 — Post-op, RR 6, sedated on opioid, SpO₂ 92% falling
- **Mechanism:** opioid blunted CO₂ drive → hypoventilation → Type 2 (CO₂↑, resp
  acidosis — Module 02).
- **Priority:** stimulate, support ventilation/airway, **naloxone**, call. RR/sedation
  caught it before SpO₂ — that's the point of Chain 4.

### Case 3 — Sudden dyspnea, sharp chest pain, SpO₂ 86%, clear lungs, tachycardic
- **Mechanism:** **PE** → dead space (clot blocks perfusion).
- **Priority:** O₂, sit up, monitor, rapid escalation/anticoagulation pathway; high-acuity.

### Case 4 — COPD, drowsy, SpO₂ 90% on 6 L; someone wants to crank O₂ to 15 L
- **Mechanism:** chronic retainer — over-oxygenation can worsen CO₂ retention/somnolence.
- **Priority:** **titrate to the ordered target (~88–92%)**, do NOT withhold needed O₂,
  watch for rising CO₂/drowsiness, get an ABG, escalate. Balanced answer = correct answer.

### Case 5 — Pneumonia, crackles, SpO₂ 89%, febrile
- **Mechanism:** alveoli fill → shunt + thickened membrane (Fick).
- **Priority:** O₂ to target, positioning (good lung down varies — high-Fowler's for ease
  of breathing), antibiotics/cultures, hydration, incentive spirometry, monitor.

## Transferable move
Cue → oxygenation vs ventilation problem? → shunt/dead space/obstruction/drive? →
**Airway-Breathing priority** action. Same engine, respiratory domain.
