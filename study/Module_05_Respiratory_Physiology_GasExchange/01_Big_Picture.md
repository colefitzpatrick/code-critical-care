# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **The lung is a gas exchanger: it must move air (ventilation) AND match that air to
> blood flow (perfusion) to load O₂ and dump CO₂ — two separate jobs that fail
> separately.**

## The two axes (the whole module)
| Job | Gas it controls | Measured by | Fails as |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Ventilation** | CO₂ | PaCO₂ (ABG), RR/effort | Type 2 failure → respiratory acidosis (Mod 02) |
| **Oxygenation** | O₂ | SpO₂, PaO₂ | Type 1 failure → hypoxemia |

> A patient can fail one, the other, or both. "Sats are fine" ≠ "ventilation is fine."

## Engineering translation
| Physiology | Your language |
|---|---|
| Diffusion (alveolus↔blood) | Fick: flux ∝ area × ΔP / thickness |
| V/Q matching | Match feed (air) to flow (blood) for efficient transfer |
| Shunt | Blood bypasses gas exchange (perfused, not ventilated) |
| Dead space | Air that never meets blood (ventilated, not perfused) |
| O₂–Hb curve | A saturable binding isotherm; right shift = easier unload |

## The spine
```
Drive to breathe (mostly CO₂/pH) → ventilation moves air to alveoli
        ↓ must match
Perfusion (blood) at the alveoli  → DIFFUSION loads O₂ / unloads CO₂
        ↓ carried by
Hemoglobin (O₂–Hb curve) → tissues → (Module 01 ATP)
        ↓ break a step
hypoxemia and/or hypercapnia → predictable ABG → predictable nurse action
```

## 5 anchors
1. Ventilation = CO₂; oxygenation = O₂. Different problems, different fixes.
2. Efficient exchange needs **V matched to Q**.
3. **Shunt** → hypoxemia that **does not** correct with more O₂ (key ARDS idea).
4. Main breathing drive = rising CO₂/acidity (central chemoreceptors).
5. Airway/Breathing is **always** the first ABC priority.
