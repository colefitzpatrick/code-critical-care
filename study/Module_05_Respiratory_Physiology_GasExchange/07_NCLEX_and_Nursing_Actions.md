# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Respiratory is the **#1 NCLEX priority topic** because Airway/Breathing beats everything.
Do this file twice.

## A. The priority rule
**Airway → Breathing → Circulation.** A respiratory item almost always outranks others.
- A patient who **can't maintain an airway / is in distress** = act *now* (don't "assess
  more" first — this is the emergency exception).
- "Which patient do you see first?" → the new/worsening **respiratory** one.
- Silent chest / no air movement / one-word sentences / stridor = highest acuity.

## B. Assessment priorities
- Rate, rhythm, **effort/work of breathing** (accessory muscles, retractions, tripod),
  SpO₂ **and** mental status (drowsy = CO₂ or hypoxia), breath sounds, color, ABG.
- **Trend > single value.** A falling RR after opioids is worse than a one-off SpO₂.
- Capnography/RR detects hypoventilation **before** SpO₂ drops.

## C. Oxygen delivery (low → high) + when
| Device | Rough FiO₂ | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Nasal cannula | ~24–44% (1–6 L) | mild, stable, eating |
| Simple mask | ~40–60% | moderate |
| Non-rebreather | ~60–95% | emergency/severe hypoxia |
| High-flow nasal / NIV (BiPAP/CPAP) | high | failing, pre-intubation |
| Intubation + ventilator | controlled | failure / can't protect airway |
> COPD retainers: **titrate to the ordered target (~88–92%)**, don't withhold needed O₂.

## D. Core nurse actions (by pattern)
- **Hypoxia anyone:** position upright (high-Fowler's), O₂ to target, calm, reassess,
  escalate. Position changes & oxygen are independent nursing actions.
- **Shunt/ARDS:** anticipate PEEP/vent/prone; more FiO₂ alone won't fix it.
- **Hypoventilation (opioid):** stimulate, support ventilation, naloxone.
- **Secretions/atelectasis:** cough/deep breathe, **incentive spirometry**, suction PRN,
  ambulate, hydrate.
- **Acute distress:** stay, call for help, prepare for escalation — don't leave the room.

## E. Labs/monitoring you act on
- **ABG** (Module 02 algorithm): pH, PaCO₂ (ventilation), PaO₂ (oxygenation), HCO₃⁻.
- **SpO₂** = Hb saturation only (not delivery; misleading in anemia/CO poisoning/poor perfusion).
- Trend RR + sedation score with opioids.

## F. Patient teaching (NCLEX "shows understanding")
- **Incentive spirometer:** inhale slowly/deeply, hold ~3–5 s, ~10×/hr while awake
  (post-op atelectasis prevention).
- **COPD:** pursed-lip breathing (slows exhalation, prevents airway collapse), energy
  conservation, take O₂ as prescribed (don't crank it up), vaccines, smoking cessation.
- **Asthma:** use the **bronchodilator (rescue) inhaler before the steroid**, spacer,
  rinse mouth after steroid, recognize early worsening, peak-flow zones.
- **Smoking cessation** = the single highest-yield teaching for almost every resp patient.

## G. Safety & delegation
- Unstable airway/breathing, titrating O₂ on the unstable, first assessment, teaching,
  interpreting ABG = **RN**.
- UAP **can**: reposition, apply prescribed stable O₂, ambulate stable patients, vitals
  on stable patients, set up incentive spirometer (RN teaches/evaluates).
- Always: HOB up for dyspnea, suction/airway equipment available, continuous SpO₂ for the
  unstable, never leave a patient in acute distress.

## H. NCLEX test-taking on respiratory
- "First action" in distress = **act on airway/breathing** (position/O₂/call), not
  "document" or "reassess in an hour."
- Worsening RR/effort/mental status > a single SpO₂ number.
- Don't withhold O₂ from a hypoxic COPD patient (titrate, don't refuse).
- Refractory hypoxia on high O₂ → escalate (shunt), not "increase O₂ and recheck later."
