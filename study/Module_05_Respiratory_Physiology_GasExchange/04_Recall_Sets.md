<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. CO₂ / O₂; CO₂ by PaCO₂ & RR/effort, O₂ by SpO₂/PaO₂.
- A2. Type 1 = oxygenation (↓O₂, CO₂ normal/low) e.g. pneumonia/ARDS/PE. Type 2 = ventilation (↑CO₂ → resp acidosis) e.g. opioid OD/COPD.
- A3. SpO₂ is the O₂ axis only; CO₂ can climb (Type 2) with adequate sats — must check RR/sedation/ABG.

**B**
- B1. Shunt = perfused, not ventilated (ARDS/pneumonia). Dead space = ventilated, not perfused (PE).
- B2. The extra O₂ never reaches the blood passing unventilated alveoli — it's bypassed.
- B3. Area (emphysema), ΔP (low FiO₂/altitude), thickness (edema/fibrosis/pneumonia).

**C**
- C1. Rising CO₂/↓pH (central chemoreceptors); opioids/sedatives blunt it; naloxone reverses.
- C2. Fever, acidosis, ↑CO₂, ↑2,3-BPG, exercise → easier O₂ unloading to tissues.
- C3. ARDS/pneumonia = shunt; PE = dead space; COPD/asthma = obstruction (Type 2 tendency).
</details>
