# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — Two jobs, two failures
Ventilation clears **CO₂**; oxygenation loads **O₂** — independent
**→ Type 1 failure:** ↓O₂, CO₂ normal/low (oxygenation problem — pneumonia, ARDS, PE)
**→ Type 2 failure:** ↑CO₂ → **respiratory acidosis** (ventilation problem — opioid OD,
COPD, fatigue) — *this is the Module 02 ABG link*
**→ nurse:** SpO₂ tells the O₂ side; **RR/effort/ABG** tells the CO₂ side. A sleepy COPD
patient with "okay sats" can be silently retaining CO₂ → check ventilation, not just SpO₂.

## Chain 2 — V/Q matching is everything
Efficient gas exchange needs ventilation (V) matched to perfusion (Q)
**→ Shunt** (perfused but NOT ventilated — flooded/collapsed alveoli: ARDS, pneumonia):
blood passes unventilated → **hypoxemia that barely improves with more O₂** (the O₂
never reaches that blood)
**→ Dead space** (ventilated but NOT perfused — e.g., **PE** blocks flow): wasted
breaths, CO₂ can't offload
**→ nurse:** refractory low SpO₂ on high O₂ = think shunt → escalate (positioning, PEEP/
vent, treat cause); don't just keep cranking the FiO₂ and walk away.

## Chain 3 — Diffusion (your Fick chain)
O₂/CO₂ cross the alveolar membrane by diffusion: flux ∝ area × ΔP ÷ thickness
**→** thicken it (edema, fibrosis, pneumonia) or lose area (emphysema) → impaired exchange
**→ nurse:** this is *why* pulmonary edema and pneumonia drop SpO₂ — the membrane got
thick/wet. Treat the cause (diuresis in cardiogenic edema, antibiotics in pneumonia).

## Chain 4 — Control of breathing
Central chemoreceptors sense **rising CO₂/falling pH** → main drive to breathe
**→** opioids/sedatives blunt this drive → hypoventilation → CO₂↑ → Type 2 failure
**→ nurse:** monitor sedation + RR after opioids; **naloxone** reverses it (Module 02
opioid case). Capnography/RR catches it before SpO₂ falls.

## Chain 5 — O₂–hemoglobin curve & delivery
Most O₂ rides on hemoglobin (Hb). The curve is saturable; a **right shift** (fever,
acidosis, ↑CO₂, exercise) = Hb releases O₂ to tissues more easily — exactly where
metabolism is high
**→ nurse:** SpO₂ is Hb saturation, not O₂ delivery. Anemia or poor perfusion = low
delivery despite "normal" SpO₂ (link Mod 01 hypoxia types: anemic/stagnant).

## Chain 6 — The big patho patterns (one line each)
- **ARDS** = widespread shunt → refractory hypoxemia; needs PEEP/vent.
- **COPD/asthma** = airflow obstruction → air trapping, CO₂ retention (Type 2 tendency).
- **PE** = dead space (clot blocks Q) → hypoxemia + sudden dyspnea.
- **Pneumonia** = alveoli fill → shunt + thick membrane → hypoxemia.
**→ nurse:** the pattern predicts the action (below + file 07).

## Chain 7 — The COPD O₂ nuance (done correctly)
In some chronic CO₂-retainers, high-flow O₂ can worsen V/Q matching & CO₂ retention →
rising CO₂/somnolence
**→ nurse:** **titrate O₂ to the ordered SpO₂ target (often ~88–92% in COPD)** — do NOT
withhold needed O₂; give what's required and **watch for rising CO₂/drowsiness**, escalate.
*(Hypoxia kills faster than hypercapnia — treat hypoxia, just don't over-oxygenate blindly.)*

---
### Compression (20 s)
> Ventilation = CO₂, oxygenation = O₂; they fail separately (Type 2 = resp acidosis).
> Efficient exchange needs V matched to Q; shunt = hypoxemia O₂ won't fix. Diffusion is
> Fick. CO₂ drives breathing. Every pattern → an airway/oxygen nurse action.
