# _GLOSSARY — Module 02 (plain English)

A lookup, not a read-through. Bold terms in the other files are defined here.
Format: **term** — plain meaning — *(engineering link)*.

## Group A — Fluids & volume
- **Hypovolemia** — too little fluid in the tank (bleeding, vomiting, diarrhea). Low BP, fast HR.
- **Hypervolemia** — too much fluid (heart/kidney failure, over-infusion). Edema, lung crackles.
- **Edema** — excess fluid in the tissue space between cells (visible, pitting). *(Mod 01 term.)*
- **Diuretic** — a drug that makes the kidney excrete water + Na⁺ (drains the tank).
- **Turgor** — skin elasticity; poor turgor ("tenting") suggests low volume.
- **Daily weight** — best single volume gauge; ~1 L fluid ≈ 1 kg.

## Group B — Electrolytes
- **Hyponatremia / hypernatremia** — low / high serum sodium = too dilute / too concentrated (a *water* ratio, not total salt).
- **Hypokalemia / hyperkalemia** — low / high potassium (cardiac danger — Mod 01 Ch4).
- **Hypocalcemia / hypercalcemia** — low / high calcium. Low = hyper-excitable nerves.
- **Tetany** — involuntary muscle spasm from low Ca²⁺ (hyper-excitable membranes).
- **Trousseau / Chvostek signs** — bedside tests for low Ca²⁺ (BP-cuff hand spasm / facial-tap twitch).
- **Laryngospasm** — the voice-box muscles clamp the airway shut — an emergency.
- **Magnesium (Mg²⁺)** — tracks with K⁺; low Mg²⁺ → stubborn low K⁺ + torsades.

## Group C — Acid–base machinery
- **pH** — how acidic the blood is; normal **7.35–7.45**. Low = acidemia, high = alkalemia.
- **Bicarbonate (HCO₃⁻)** — the **base** term; the kidney controls it (slow). Normal 22–26.
- **PaCO₂** — dissolved CO₂ in arterial blood = the **acid** term; lungs control it (fast). Normal 35–45.
- **Buffer** — a weak-acid/base pair that absorbs added acid/base to resist pH change.
- **Henderson–Hasselbalch** — pH = pKa + log(base/acid); here pH ∝ HCO₃⁻ / PaCO₂.
- **Le Chatelier** — add to one side of an equilibrium, it shifts to consume the addition (how the buffer + lungs respond).
- **Anion gap** — a calculated number that flags *which kind* of metabolic acidosis (high = lactate/ketones/toxins; normal = diarrhea/renal). Normal 8–12.

## Group D — Disorders, controllers & actions
- **Respiratory acidosis/alkalosis** — pH problem from CO₂ (lungs): ↑CO₂→acid, ↓CO₂→alkali.
- **Metabolic acidosis/alkalosis** — pH problem from HCO₃⁻: ↓→acid, ↑→alkali.
- **Compensation** — the *other* controller shifting pH back **toward** (never past) normal.
- **ROME** — Respiratory = pH/CO₂ Opposite · Metabolic = pH/HCO₃⁻ Equal.
- **Kussmaul breathing** — deep, fast breathing = lungs compensating for metabolic acidosis (DKA).
- **DKA (diabetic ketoacidosis)** — no insulin → ketoacids → high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis.
- **Naloxone** — reverses opioids; fixes opioid-caused respiratory acidosis by restoring breathing.
- **ABG (arterial blood gas)** — blood test for pH, PaCO₂, HCO₃⁻, O₂ — the acid–base decision tool.
- **Osmotic demyelination** — brain injury from correcting low sodium too fast (why we go slow).
