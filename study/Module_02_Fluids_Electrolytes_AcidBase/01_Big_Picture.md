# 01 · The Big Picture (start broad)

## The one-sentence model
> **The body is a buffered tank of salt water held at a regulated volume and a tightly
> controlled pH by two controllers — a fast one (lungs) and a slow one (kidneys).**

Three regulated things, same control-loop logic from Module 01:
1. **Volume** — how full the tank is (water + Na⁺ travel together).
2. **Composition** — the key ions (Na⁺, K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) each in a tight band.
3. **pH** — how acidic the water is (7.35–7.45), defended by a buffer + 2 controllers.

## Engineering translation (your edge)
| Physiology | Your language |
|---|---|
| Bicarbonate buffer | A weak-acid/conjugate-base buffer system |
| Henderson–Hasselbalch | pH = pKa + log(base/acid) — a log ratio |
| Lungs adjust CO₂ | Fast actuator (seconds–min), changes the *acid* term |
| Kidneys adjust HCO₃⁻ | Slow actuator (hours–days), changes the *base* term |
| Compensation | The *other* loop correcting toward set point (never overshoots) |
| Na⁺ & water | Coupled variables — Na⁺ is the osmotic skeleton water hangs on |

## The core logic chain (the spine)
```
Water follows Na⁺ (osmosis, Module 01)            ← controls VOLUME
        ↓
Cells/nerves/heart need each ion in a tight band  ← controls COMPOSITION
        ↓
Metabolism constantly makes acid (CO₂ + fixed acids)
        ↓ must be neutralized
Bicarbonate BUFFER absorbs it instantly (CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻)
        ↓ then two controllers fine-tune pH
LUNGS (fast): blow off / retain CO₂   |   KIDNEYS (slow): excrete / keep HCO₃⁻
        ↓ break any of these
predictable disorder → predictable lab → predictable nurse action
```

## The 5 ideas everything links back to
1. **Na⁺ = water.** Sodium problems are usually *water* problems. Volume rides on Na⁺.
2. **K⁺ lives mostly inside cells** — small serum shifts = big cardiac effects (Mod 01 Ch4).
3. **pH = base/acid ratio.** pH ∝ HCO₃⁻ (kidney/metabolic) ÷ PaCO₂ (lung/respiratory).
4. **Two controllers, two speeds.** Lungs fix pH in minutes; kidneys in days.
5. **Acid–base and K⁺ are coupled.** H⁺ and K⁺ trade places across the cell membrane.
