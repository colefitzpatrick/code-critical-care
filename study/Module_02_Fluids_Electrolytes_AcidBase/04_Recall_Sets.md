<details><summary>ANSWER KEY — open only after answering</summary>

**Set A**
- A1. Water follows Na⁺ osmotically, so total-body Na⁺ tracks volume; serum Na⁺ reflects the Na⁺-to-**water** ratio (concentration), not total salt.
- A2. ↓BP, ↑HR, dry membranes, ↓urine, weight↓, poor skin turgor — best monitor: **daily weight** (+ I/O).
- A3. Edema, lung crackles, ↑weight, ↑BP, distended neck veins → restrict fluid/Na⁺, anticipate **diuretics**, monitor.
- A4. Low Na⁺ = dilute ECF → water moves **into** brain cells → swelling → confusion/**seizures**.
- A5. Brain water shifts dangerously if pH/osmolality corrected fast (osmotic demyelination, or cerebral edema).

**Set B**
- B1. Acidosis → H⁺ in, K⁺ out → **hyperkalemia**. Alkalosis → **hypokalemia**.
- B2. IV-push K⁺ is lethal (cardiac arrest); give diluted/controlled, on a monitor. Also check **Mg²⁺**.
- B3. Hypocalcemia → hyperexcitable nerves → **laryngospasm** (airway) + seizures. Signs: **Trousseau** & **Chvostek**.
- B4. Refractory **hypokalemia** + **torsades** (lethal arrhythmia).

**Set C**
- C1. CO₂ + H₂O ⇌ H₂CO₃ ⇌ H⁺ + HCO₃⁻.
- C2. pH ∝ **HCO₃⁻ (kidney/metabolic)** / **PaCO₂ (lungs/respiratory)**.
- C3. Shifts **left** (consumes H⁺ → forms CO₂); lungs **blow off the CO₂** (Le Chatelier).
- C4. Lungs **fast** (seconds–minutes); kidneys **slow** (hours–days).

**Set D**
- D1. **R**espiratory = pH/CO₂ **O**pposite; **M**etabolic = pH/HCO₃⁻ **E**qual. Distinguishes respiratory vs metabolic.
- D2. Resp acidosis ↑PaCO₂ (opioid OD); resp alkalosis ↓PaCO₂ (anxiety/PE); metabolic acidosis ↓HCO₃⁻ (shock/DKA); metabolic alkalosis ↑HCO₃⁻ (vomiting).
- D3. pH side → PaCO₂ explains? → HCO₃⁻ explains? → compensating? → (if met. acidosis) anion gap.
- D4. pH↓ + PaCO₂↑ (pH & CO₂ opposite = Respiratory), HCO₃⁻ normal → **uncompensated respiratory acidosis** → support ventilation, find cause (opioid? → naloxone), airway.
- D5. pH↓ + HCO₃⁻↓ (equal = Metabolic) + high anion gap + lactate 6 → **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis from shock** → real fix = **restore perfusion / treat the cause**, not bicarb.
