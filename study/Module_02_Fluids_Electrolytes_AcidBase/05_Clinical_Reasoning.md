### Case 1 ‚ÄĒ Vomiting for 3 days, weak, K‚Āļ 3.0
- **Reasoning:** vomiting loses gastric acid (H‚Āļ) ‚Üí **metabolic alkalosis**; alkalosis
  drives K‚Āļ into cells ‚Üí **hypokalemia**; also volume loss (Na‚Āļ/water out).
- **Expect ABG:** pH‚ÜĎ, HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ‚ÜĎ (Metabolic, Equal). 
- **Action:** isotonic fluid + Cl‚ĀĽ + **K‚Āļ replacement (never push, on monitor)**, check
  Mg¬≤‚Āļ, treat the vomiting. *Two Module-02 chains (acid‚Äďbase + K‚Āļ coupling) in one patient.*

### Case 2 ‚ÄĒ Opioid given, RR 6, sleepy, then unresponsive
- **Reasoning:** opioids depress the respiratory drive ‚Üí hypoventilation ‚Üí CO‚āā retained
  ‚Üí **respiratory acidosis**.
- **Expect ABG:** pH‚Üď, PaCO‚āā‚ÜĎ (Respiratory, Opposite), HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ normal (acute, no time to
  compensate).
- **Action:** stimulate/support breathing, **bag-mask/airway**, give **naloxone** (opioid
  reversal), call for help. Fix is ventilation ‚ÄĒ the CO‚āā leaves when air moves.

### Case 3 ‚ÄĒ DKA: thirsty, deep fast breathing, glucose very high
- **Reasoning:** no insulin ‚Üí ketoacids ‚Üí **high-anion-gap metabolic acidosis**;
  **Kussmaul breathing** = lungs compensating (blow off CO‚āā, fast loop). Total-body K‚Āļ
  is low even if serum K‚Āļ looks normal (acidosis masks it).
- **Expect ABG:** pH‚Üď, HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ‚Üď, PaCO‚āā‚Üď (compensation), high anion gap.
- **Action:** IV fluids + insulin per protocol; **watch K‚Āļ obsessively** ‚ÄĒ it crashes as
  insulin/correction drives K‚Āļ into cells (Chain 3 in reverse). Monitor.

### Case 4 ‚ÄĒ Septic shock patient, lactate 6, BP low (links Module 01)
- **Reasoning:** hypoperfusion ‚Üí anaerobic metabolism ‚Üí lactate ‚Üí **high-anion-gap
  metabolic acidosis**; acidosis pushes K‚Āļ out ‚Üí watch for **hyperkalemia**.
- **Expect ABG:** pH‚Üď, HCO‚āÉ‚ĀĽ‚Üď, PaCO‚āā‚Üď (respiratory compensation), high anion gap, ‚ÜĎlactate.
- **Action:** this is the Module 01 spiral ‚ÄĒ **fix perfusion** (sepsis Hour-1 bundle,
  vasopressors to MAP‚Č•65). The acidosis resolves when perfusion does; bicarb chases a
  number, not the cause.

### Case 5 ‚ÄĒ Anxious post-op patient, RR 30, tingling fingers, normal O‚āā
- **Reasoning:** hyperventilation blows off too much CO‚āā ‚Üí **respiratory alkalosis**;
  alkalosis ‚Üí transient ‚Üď ionized Ca¬≤‚Āļ ‚Üí tingling/tetany.
- **Expect ABG:** pH‚ÜĎ, PaCO‚āā‚Üď (Respiratory, Opposite).
- **Action:** treat the *cause* ‚ÄĒ pain/anxiety; coach slow breathing; rule out PE if it
  doesn't fit. Don't just "calm down" ‚ÄĒ find why they're hyperventilating.
