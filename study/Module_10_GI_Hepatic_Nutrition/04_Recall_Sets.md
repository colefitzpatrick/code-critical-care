<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. Detox (ammonia→urea/drugs), synthesis (albumin + clotting factors), bile, glucose storage, portal blood filtering.
- A2. Varices (bleed), ascites, splenomegaly (+ encephalopathy from impaired detox).
- A3. No clotting-factor synthesis (↑INR) + low platelets (splenic sequestration).
- A4. ↑ammonia (failed detox); **lactulose**, titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day.

**B**
- B1. ABCs, 2 large-bore IVs, fluids/blood, type & cross, monitor for hypovolemic shock, find/stop source.
- B2. Octreotide (↓splanchnic flow), endoscopic banding/cautery, PPI; protect airway if massive.
- B3. Gallstones/alcohol; ↑lipase/amylase; NPO + aggressive IV fluids + pain control, watch hypocalcemia.

**C**
- C1. "If the gut works, use it" — enteral preferred; parenteral (TPN) when gut unusable.
- C2. ↓PO₄, ↓K⁺, ↓Mg²⁺ (insulin surge drives them into cells) → feed slow, replace, monitor.
- C3. HOB ≥30°, verify placement, check tolerance/residuals per policy.
