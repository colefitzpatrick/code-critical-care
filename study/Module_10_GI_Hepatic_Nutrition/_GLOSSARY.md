## Liver
- **Cirrhosis** — chronic liver scarring → loss of function + portal hypertension.
- **Portal hypertension** — high pressure in the portal vein from a scarred liver → varices, ascites, splenomegaly.
- **Esophageal/gastric varices** — swollen fragile collateral veins that can hemorrhage massively.
- **Ascites** — fluid in the abdomen (portal HTN + low albumin).
- **Paracentesis** — draining ascitic fluid with a needle.
- **Hepatic encephalopathy** — confusion/↓LOC from ammonia the failing liver can't clear.
- **Ammonia** — toxic waste normally converted to urea by the liver.
- **Asterixis** — flapping hand tremor of hepatic encephalopathy.
- **Lactulose** — drug that traps ammonia in the gut and removes it (titrate to 2–3 stools/day).
- **Coagulopathy** — bleeding tendency (liver can't make clotting factors → ↑INR).
- **Albumin** — liver-made protein that holds fluid in vessels; low → ascites/edema.
- **Jaundice** — yellow skin/eyes from high bilirubin.
- **Hepatitis** — liver inflammation (viral A/B/C, alcohol, drugs).

## GI bleed / pancreas
- **Hematemesis** — vomiting blood (upper GI bleed). **Melena** — black tarry stool (digested blood).
- **Octreotide** — drug that lowers splanchnic blood flow in variceal bleeding.
- **PPI** — proton pump inhibitor (acid suppression, ulcers).
- **Pancreatitis** — pancreatic enzymes autodigest the pancreas; ↑lipase/amylase.
- **Lipase / amylase** — pancreatic enzymes; elevated in pancreatitis.

## Nutrition
- **Enteral nutrition** — feeding via the GI tract (oral/NG/PEG tube). Preferred if gut works.
- **Parenteral nutrition (TPN)** — IV nutrition when the gut can't be used; via central line.
- **Refeeding syndrome** — feeding a starved patient → insulin surge → ↓PO₄/↓K⁺/↓Mg²⁺ + fluid shifts; can be fatal.
- **Residual** — stomach contents checked to assess tube-feed tolerance.
