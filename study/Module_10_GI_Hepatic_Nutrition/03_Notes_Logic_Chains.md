# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — The liver as chemical plant
Liver detoxifies (ammonia→urea, drugs), synthesizes (**albumin**, **clotting factors**), makes bile, stores glucose, filters portal blood
**→ lose it (cirrhosis):** every job fails together
**→ nurse:** expect bleeding (low factors), edema/ascites (low albumin), confusion (ammonia), jaundice, drug accumulation (dose carefully); monitor **AST/ALT, bilirubin, albumin, ammonia, INR**.

## Chain 2 — Portal hypertension (the cirrhosis hub)
Scarred liver blocks portal blood flow → back-pressure (**portal hypertension**)
**→** blood detours through fragile collateral veins → **esophageal/gastric varices** (rupture = massive bleed) + **splenomegaly** + **ascites** (high pressure + low albumin push fluid into the belly)
**→ nurse:** varices + coagulopathy = a bleed waiting to happen. Watch for hematemesis/melena; **non-selective beta-blockers** lower portal pressure (prevention); ascites → sodium/fluid restriction, **paracentesis**, monitor respiratory effort.

## Chain 3 — Hepatic encephalopathy (ammonia)
Liver can't convert **ammonia** → urea → ammonia rises → crosses to brain → confusion, asterixis, ↓LOC, coma
**→ nurse:** **lactulose** (traps ammonia in the gut, dumps it in stool — titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day), ± rifaximin; monitor LOC and ammonia; assess for precipitants (GI bleed, infection, constipation).

## Chain 4 — Coagulopathy + GI bleed = the deadly combo
No clotting-factor synthesis → **↑INR, bleeding tendency**; plus low platelets (splenic sequestration)
**→** a variceal bleed in a patient who can't clot → **hypovolemic shock** fast (Mod 3)
**→ nurse:** GI bleed = ABCs first — **two large-bore IVs, fluids/blood, type & cross**, monitor for shock; anticipate **octreotide** (↓splanchnic flow), **endoscopy** (band/cauterize), PPI; protect airway if massive hematemesis.

## Chain 5 — Pancreatitis
Pancreatic enzymes activate early and **autodigest** the pancreas (gallstones/alcohol #1 causes)
**→** severe epigastric pain (radiates to back), **↑lipase/amylase**, nausea; can third-space fluid → shock; hypocalcemia
**→ nurse:** **NPO** (rest the pancreas), IV fluids (aggressive — they third-space), pain control, monitor for complications (necrosis, ARDS — Mod 6, hypocalcemia — Mod 2).

## Chain 6 — Nutrition: enteral vs parenteral
"**If the gut works, use it**" — enteral feeding preserves the gut barrier and is safer than IV
**→ Parenteral (TPN)** = IV nutrition when the gut can't be used; needs central line; infection/glucose risks
**→ nurse:** verify NG/feeding tube placement, HOB ≥30° (aspiration), check residuals/tolerance per policy; TPN → strict glucose monitoring, central-line care, never stop abruptly (rebound hypoglycemia).

## Chain 7 — Refeeding syndrome (Mod 2 link)
Starved body shifts to fat metabolism → you feed carbs → **insulin surges** → drives **phosphate, potassium, magnesium INTO cells** → dangerous **↓PO₄, ↓K⁺, ↓Mg²⁺** + fluid shifts → arrhythmia, weakness
**→ nurse:** in malnourished/NPO-long patients, **start feeding SLOWLY**, check and replace **PO₄/K⁺/Mg²⁺** before and during, monitor closely. Going too fast can be fatal.

---
### Compression (20 s)
> Liver = detox + synthesis + bile + storage + portal filter. Cirrhosis → portal HTN →
> varices (bleed) + ascites + encephalopathy (ammonia→lactulose) + coagulopathy. GI bleed
> = hypovolemic shock (ABCs, blood, scope). Pancreatitis = autodigestion (NPO, fluids).
> Refeeding = ↓PO₄/K/Mg — feed slow.
