# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → mechanism → priority action.**

### Case 1 — Cirrhosis patient vomiting bright red blood, BP 84/50, HR 122
- **Mechanism:** **variceal hemorrhage** → hypovolemic shock; can't clot (coagulopathy).
- **Priority:** ABCs — protect airway, 2 large-bore IVs, fluids/blood, type & cross, anticipate octreotide + emergent endoscopy. This is a true emergency.

### Case 2 — Cirrhosis patient: confused, flapping tremor (asterixis), constipated
- **Mechanism:** **hepatic encephalopathy** (↑ammonia, precipitated by constipation/bleed).
- **Priority:** **lactulose** (titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day), find precipitant, safety/neuro checks, monitor ammonia/LOC.

### Case 3 — Severe epigastric pain radiating to back, lipase 5×, nausea, alcohol use
- **Mechanism:** **acute pancreatitis** (autodigestion).
- **Priority:** **NPO**, aggressive IV fluids, pain control, monitor for complications (hypocalcemia, ARDS, shock).

### Case 4 — Malnourished patient, NPO 10 days, just started tube feeds; now PO₄ 1.2, K⁺ 2.9
- **Mechanism:** **refeeding syndrome** — insulin surge drove electrolytes into cells.
- **Priority:** slow/hold feeds, **replace PO₄/K⁺/Mg²⁺**, cardiac monitor, advance nutrition slowly.

### Case 5 — Cirrhosis with tense distended abdomen, short of breath
- **Mechanism:** **ascites** (portal HTN + low albumin) splinting the diaphragm.
- **Priority:** HOB up, sodium/fluid restriction, anticipate **paracentesis**, monitor respiratory status + weight/girth.

## Transferable move
Cue → liver job lost / GI bleed / pancreatic / nutrition? → predict the labs → act
(ABCs for bleeds, lactulose for ammonia, slow feeds for refeeding).
