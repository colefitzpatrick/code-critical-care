# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Do twice. GI bleeds and liver failure are favorite NCLEX priority scenarios.

## A. Priority rule
**GI bleed = ABC + shock** (it outranks most things). Airway first if massive hematemesis or ↓LOC from encephalopathy.

## B. Assessment priorities
- **GI bleed:** vitals/shock signs, hematemesis/melena, Hgb/Hct trend, abdomen, orthostatics.
- **Liver failure:** LOC + **asterixis** (encephalopathy), abdominal girth/weight (ascites), bleeding/bruising (coagulopathy), jaundice, ammonia.
- **Pancreatitis:** pain, lipase/amylase, Ca²⁺, respiratory status (ARDS risk).
- **Tube feeds:** placement, HOB, tolerance/residuals, glucose (TPN).

## C. Drugs → why → watch
| Drug | Use | Watch |
|---|---|---|
| **Lactulose** | hepatic encephalopathy (dumps ammonia) | titrate to 2–3 soft stools/day; dehydration/↓K⁺ if overdone |
| Rifaximin | gut bacteria → less ammonia | adjunct to lactulose |
| **Octreotide** | variceal bleed (↓splanchnic flow) | BP, glucose |
| Non-selective beta-blocker (propranolol) | lower portal pressure (varices prophylaxis) | HR/BP |
| PPI (pantoprazole) | ulcers / acid suppression | — |
| Spironolactone ± furosemide | ascites diuresis | K⁺ (opposite effects), volume |
| Pancreatic enzymes (chronic) | replace enzymes | take with food |
| **Vitamin K / FFP** | reverse coagulopathy (liver) | bleeding/INR |

## D. Labs you act on
- **AST/ALT** (hepatocyte injury) · **bilirubin** (jaundice) · **albumin** (synthesis/edema) · **ammonia** (encephalopathy) · **INR/PT** (coagulopathy) · **lipase/amylase** (pancreatitis) · **Hgb/Hct** (bleed) · **PO₄/K⁺/Mg²⁺** (refeeding).

## E. Teaching
- **Cirrhosis:** avoid alcohol + hepatotoxins (acetaminophen limits), low-sodium diet, daily weight/girth, report bleeding/confusion, lactulose adherence (yes, the diarrhea is the point).
- **Tube feeds/TPN:** HOB up, oral care, report intolerance; TPN never stopped abruptly.
- **Hepatitis prevention:** vaccines (A/B), no needle sharing, safe food/water.

## F. Safety & delegation
- **RN:** GI-bleed/shock management, tube-feed verification, blood products, teaching.
- **UAP can:** I&O/daily weight, vitals (report), ADLs, stoma/peri care for stable — RN evaluates.
- **High alert:** blood transfusions (verify, monitor reactions — Mod 11), insulin with TPN.

## G. NCLEX test-taking
- Cirrhosis + confusion + asterixis = **hepatic encephalopathy → lactulose** (more lactulose if not stooling).
- Variceal bleed = airway + volume first; **octreotide + scope**.
- Refeeding syndrome → **low PO₄/K⁺/Mg²⁺**; feed slowly.
- "If the gut works, use it" — enteral over parenteral.
- Liver patient + acetaminophen = caution (hepatotoxic); liver patient bleeds (give vit K/FFP, not more anticoagulant).
