<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. CPP = MAP − ICP; keep ≥ ~60.
- A2. Brain + blood + CSF (in a fixed skull).
- A3. Normal 7–15 mmHg; treat >20.
- A4. CO₂ dilates cerebral vessels → ↑cerebral blood volume → ↑ICP.

**B**
- B1. **↓ Level of consciousness (LOC)** — earliest + most sensitive.
- B2. ↑SBP with **widening pulse pressure** + **bradycardia** + **irregular respirations**; **LATE** (near herniation).
- B3. Shock = ↓BP + ↑HR; Cushing's = ↑BP + ↓HR — it's a brainstem reflex, not shock.
- B4. HOB 30° + midline (venous drainage), normocapnia, hyperosmolar (mannitol/3% saline), sedation, CSF drainage, treat fever, avoid hyponatremia. HOB 30° promotes venous outflow → lowers ICP.

**C**
- C1. Face droop, Arm drift, Speech difficulty, Time (call/last-known-well).
- C2. Ischemic → tPA (in window); hemorrhagic → **NO tPA** (worsens bleed).
- C3. ~3–4.5 h from last-known-well, **bleed ruled out by CT first**, BP controlled <185/110, no other contraindications.
- C4. Stroke impairs swallowing → aspiration risk; screen before any oral intake.

**D**
- D1. Do: protect head/side-lying, time it, O₂/suction after. Don't: restrain, put anything in mouth.
- D2. >5 min or recurrent without recovery; first-line = **benzodiazepine** (lorazepam/midazolam).
- D3. SIADH → retain water → **hypoNa** (cerebral edema); DI → lose water → **hyperNa**.
- D4. **Glucose** — hypoglycemia mimics stroke/altered mental status and is instantly reversible.
</details>
