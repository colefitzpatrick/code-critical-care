# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Neuro is high-yield because it links perfusion, Na⁺, CO₂, glucose, and airway. Do twice.

## A. Priority rule
**ABC + LOC.** A dropping level of consciousness is an airway threat and the earliest ↑ICP sign — it outranks most things.
- **GCS ≤ 8 → intubate** (can't protect airway).
- Sudden focal deficit = stroke alert, time-critical.
- Active seizure = safety + airway now.

## B. Assessment priorities
- **Neuro checks:** LOC (earliest ↑ICP sign), GCS, pupils (size/reactivity — a "blown" pupil = herniation sign), motor/strength, orientation.
- **Vitals pattern:** watch for **Cushing's triad** (↑SBP/wide pulse pressure, bradycardia, irregular breathing) = LATE ↑ICP.
- **Always check glucose** in altered mental status (reversible mimic).
- Na⁺ + osmolality + urine output (SIADH/DI after brain injury).

## C. ↑ICP management — action → why
| Action | Why |
|---|---|
| HOB **30°**, head **midline**, no hip flexion | Promotes venous drainage from the head → ↓ICP |
| **Normocapnia** (don't let CO₂ rise; don't over-hyperventilate) | CO₂ dilates cerebral vessels → ↑ICP |
| **Mannitol / 3% hypertonic saline** | Osmotically pulls water out of brain (Mod 01) |
| Sedation / analgesia, quiet environment, cluster care | ↓ metabolic demand + avoid ICP spikes |
| Treat fever | Fever ↑ brain metabolic demand |
| Avoid hyponatremia | Low Na⁺ → cerebral edema (Mod 02) |
| CSF drainage (EVD) | Directly removes volume from the box |

## D. Drugs → why → watch
| Drug | Use | Watch |
|---|---|---|
| **tPA (alteplase)** | Ischemic stroke in window | **Bleeding** — strict criteria; BP <185/110; CT first; no anticoagulants/recent surgery |
| **Mannitol / 3% saline** | ↓ICP | Osmolality, Na⁺, volume status |
| **Benzodiazepine (lorazepam)** | Stop active seizure / status | Respiratory depression — monitor airway |
| **Levetiracetam / phenytoin** | Maintenance antiepileptic | Phenytoin: levels, gingival changes, many interactions |
| **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** | DI (replace ADH) | Na⁺, fluid balance |
| **Nimodipine** | Prevent vasospasm after subarachnoid hemorrhage | Hypotension |

## E. Patient/family teaching
- **Stroke FAST**: face/arm/speech/time — call 911 immediately, note last-known-well.
- **Stroke recovery:** swallow precautions (NPO until screened), aspiration prevention, rehab, secondary prevention (BP, anticoagulation for AF, statin, lifestyle).
- **Seizure safety at home:** med adherence, avoid triggers, no driving until cleared, never leave alone in water, side-lying during an event, when to call 911 (>5 min, repeated, injury).
- **Anticoagulant + stroke history:** fall/bleeding precautions.

## F. Safety & delegation
- **RN:** neuro assessment, GCS, tPA administration/monitoring, seizure management, teaching.
- **UAP can:** vitals (report), reposition with HOB precautions, ADLs for stable patients, seizure precautions setup (pad rails, suction at bedside) — RN evaluates.
- **Seizure precautions:** bed low, rails padded, suction + O₂ at bedside, nothing in mouth.
- **Aspiration precautions:** NPO until swallow screen post-stroke.

## G. NCLEX test-taking on neuro
- Earliest ↑ICP sign = **change in LOC** (not pupils, not Cushing's — those are later).
- Cushing's triad = LATE/ominous; don't "treat the high BP" like routine HTN.
- **No tPA in hemorrhagic stroke** (or before CT rules out a bleed).
- During a seizure: protect + side-lying; **never restrain or put objects in the mouth.**
- Altered mental status → **check glucose first** (cheap, fast, reversible).
- DI = high Na⁺/high urine; SIADH = low Na⁺/low urine — correct Na⁺ **slowly**.
