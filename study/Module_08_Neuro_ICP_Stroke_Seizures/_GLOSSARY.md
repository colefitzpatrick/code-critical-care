# _GLOSSARY — Module 08 (plain English)

## Pressure / perfusion
- **ICP (intracranial pressure)** — pressure inside the skull; normal 7–15 mmHg, treat >20.
- **CPP (cerebral perfusion pressure)** — net pressure driving brain blood flow = **MAP − ICP**; keep ≥ ~60.
- **Monro-Kellie doctrine** — the skull is a fixed box of brain + blood + CSF; adding volume to one raises ICP.
- **Autoregulation** — the brain's ability to keep blood flow steady across a MAP range; lost after injury.
- **Herniation** — brain tissue pushed/displaced by pressure; lethal.
- **Cushing's triad** — LATE ↑ICP sign: ↑SBP with **widening pulse pressure** + **bradycardia** + **irregular respirations**.
- **EVD (external ventricular drain)** — catheter that drains CSF to lower ICP and measure it.
- **CSF (cerebrospinal fluid)** — fluid cushioning brain/spinal cord.
- **LOC (level of consciousness)** — alertness/awareness; its decline is the EARLIEST ↑ICP sign.
- **GCS (Glasgow Coma Scale)** — 3 (worst) to 15 (normal); **≤8 → intubate**.

## Stroke
- **Ischemic stroke** — clot blocks brain artery (~87%); eligible for **tPA** in window.
- **Hemorrhagic stroke** — bleeding in/around brain; **no tPA** (worsens it).
- **tPA / alteplase** — clot-dissolving drug (thrombolytic) for ischemic stroke; strict criteria.
- **Thrombectomy** — mechanical clot removal (large-vessel ischemic stroke).
- **FAST** — Face droop, Arm drift, Speech, Time — stroke screen.
- **Last-known-well time** — when patient was last normal; drives tPA eligibility.
- **TIA (transient ischemic attack)** — temporary stroke-like deficit that resolves; warning sign.
- **Vasospasm** — artery narrowing after subarachnoid hemorrhage; nimodipine prevents.

## Seizures
- **Seizure** — abnormal synchronized neuronal firing.
- **Tonic-clonic** — generalized seizure with stiffening then jerking.
- **Status epilepticus** — seizure >5 min or repeated without recovery; emergency → benzos.
- **Post-ictal** — the confused/sleepy recovery state after a seizure.
- **Aura** — warning sensation some patients get before a seizure.

## Brain ↔ body links
- **SIADH** — too much ADH → retain water → low Na⁺ → cerebral edema.
- **DI (diabetes insipidus)** — too little ADH → dump water → high Na⁺ → high urine output.
- **Normocapnia** — normal PaCO₂; important because CO₂ changes cerebral vessel size/ICP.
- **Mannitol / 3% hypertonic saline** — osmotic agents that pull water out of brain tissue.
- **Desmopressin (DDAVP)** — synthetic ADH; treats DI.
