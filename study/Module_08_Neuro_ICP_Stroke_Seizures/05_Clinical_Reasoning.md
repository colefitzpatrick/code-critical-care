# 05 · Clinical Reasoning

**Cue → mechanism → priority action (time = brain).**

### Case 1 — TBI patient: BP 188/72, HR 48, irregular breathing, pupil blown
- **Mechanism:** **Cushing's triad** = severe ↑ICP, herniation imminent.
- **Priority:** emergency — notify neurosurgery, HOB 30°/midline, hyperosmolar therapy (mannitol/3% saline) ready, secure airway, normocapnia. This is NOT shock — don't treat the "high BP" with aggressive lowering.

### Case 2 — Sudden face droop + arm weakness + slurred speech, 1 h ago
- **Mechanism:** acute **stroke** — type unknown until CT.
- **Priority:** **stroke alert**, note **last-known-well time**, STAT non-contrast CT (rule out bleed), NPO, neuro checks, BP per protocol. If ischemic + in window + criteria met → tPA; if hemorrhagic → NO tPA.

### Case 3 — Patient on warfarin, severe headache, vomiting, now drowsy
- **Mechanism:** likely **hemorrhagic stroke** (anticoagulant + bleed signs).
- **Priority:** STAT CT, **reverse anticoagulation**, control BP, neurosurgery; absolutely **no tPA**. Protect airway as LOC drops.

### Case 4 — Generalized tonic-clonic seizure, now 6 minutes, not stopping
- **Mechanism:** **status epilepticus** — emergency (demand outstrips supply).
- **Priority:** protect/side-lying, O₂, **IV benzodiazepine** (lorazepam), check **glucose**, then antiepileptic; airway support; do NOT restrain or put anything in mouth.

### Case 5 — Post-craniotomy: urine output 600 mL/hr, Na⁺ 158, very thirsty
- **Mechanism:** **diabetes insipidus (DI)** — brain injury → loss of ADH → dumping water → hypernatremia.
- **Priority:** replace fluids, anticipate **desmopressin (DDAVP)**, monitor Na⁺/osmolality/UOP, correct **slowly**. (Contrast: SIADH would be low Na⁺ + low urine.)

## Transferable move
Cue → pressure (ICP), flow (stroke), or electrical (seizure)? → check the brain's supply lines (glucose, Na⁺, CO₂, perfusion) → time-critical action.
