# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — CPP = MAP − ICP (the master equation)
Brain blood flow needs net pressure = **MAP − ICP**
**→** ICP rises (bleed/mass/edema) → CPP falls → brain ischemia → more edema → ICP rises more (a Mod 01 **positive-feedback spiral**)
**→ nurse:** keep **CPP ≥ ~60** by *either* raising MAP (fluids/pressors as ordered) *or* lowering ICP. Never let an at-risk patient get hypotensive — low MAP starves the brain directly.

## Chain 2 — Monro-Kellie → why small bleeds kill
Skull is a fixed box (**brain + blood + CSF**). Add volume → CSF/blood shift out first (compensation), then **ICP rises steeply** (nonlinear)
**→** keeps rising → brain tissue pushed → **herniation** → death
**→ nurse:** recognize **early ↑ICP** (↓LOC — *earliest + most sensitive sign*, headache, vomiting, restlessness, pupil changes) BEFORE the late signs. ↓LOC = act now, don't wait.

## Chain 3 — Cushing's triad = LATE danger (near herniation)
Severe ↑ICP → brainstem compression → body's last-ditch reflex to perfuse the brain:
**→** **↑ systolic BP with WIDENING pulse pressure + BRADYcardia + irregular breathing**
**→ nurse:** this is **late and ominous** — herniation imminent. Emergency: notify, hyperosmolar therapy ready, airway, neurosurgery. (Don't confuse with shock, which is ↓BP + ↑HR — Cushing's is the opposite.)

## Chain 4 — ↑ICP management (every action has a "why")
**→** HOB **30°** + **head midline** (promotes venous drainage) · avoid hip flexion/neck kinks · **normocapnia** (don't let CO₂ rise — CO₂ dilates cerebral vessels → ↑ICP; don't over-hyperventilate either) · **hyperosmolar therapy** (**mannitol** or **3% hypertonic saline** pulls water out of brain — Mod 01 osmosis) · sedation/analgesia (↓ demand) · **CSF drainage** (EVD) · treat fever (raises demand) · avoid hyponatremia (causes cerebral edema — Mod 02)
**→ nurse:** cluster care to avoid ICP spikes, monitor neuro checks + pupils, strict Na⁺/osmolality, keep them normothermic.

## Chain 5 — Stroke: ischemic vs hemorrhagic (totally different treatment)
**FAST** screen (Face droop, Arm drift, Speech, Time). Then imaging decides:
**→ Ischemic** (clot, ~87%): **tPA (thrombolytic)** if within window (~3–4.5 h, strict criteria, BP must be controlled <185/110) + possible **thrombectomy**. Time = brain.
**→ Hemorrhagic** (bleed): **NO tPA** (would worsen it!) — control BP, reverse anticoagulation, neurosurgery.
**→ nurse:** rapid stroke alert, last-known-well time (drives tPA eligibility), NPO until swallow screen (aspiration risk), neuro checks, BP per protocol; **never give tPA before a bleed is ruled out by CT**.

## Chain 6 — Seizures & status epilepticus
Abnormal synchronized neuronal firing → focal or generalized (e.g., **tonic-clonic**)
**→ Status epilepticus** = seizure >5 min or back-to-back without recovery = **emergency** (brain O₂/glucose demand outstrips supply → injury)
**→ nurse DURING:** protect head, **side-lying** (airway/aspiration), **do NOT restrain or put anything in the mouth**, time it, note features, lower to floor; **AFTER:** O₂, suction, recovery position, the patient is **post-ictal** (confused/sleepy — reorient).
**→ status:** **benzodiazepines** (lorazepam/midazolam) first-line → then antiepileptic (levetiracetam/phenytoin); airway/glucose check.

## Chain 7 — The brain ↔ everything-else links
**→** **Na⁺ (Mod 02/07):** brain injury causes **SIADH** (retain water → hypoNa → cerebral edema) or **DI** (lose water → hyperNa); both dangerous → monitor Na⁺ + urine output + osmolality, correct **slowly**.
**→** **CO₂ (Mod 05/06):** hypoventilation → ↑CO₂ → cerebral vasodilation → ↑ICP → keep ventilation adequate.
**→** **glucose (Mod 09):** brain runs on glucose; hypoglycemia mimics stroke/altered mental status → **always check a glucose** in any altered patient.
**→ nurse:** in any neuro change, check **glucose, Na⁺, O₂/CO₂, and perfusion** — the brain is the downstream victim of all of them.
