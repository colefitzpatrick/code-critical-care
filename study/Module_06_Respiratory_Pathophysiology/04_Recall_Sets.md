<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. Obstruction (asthma/COPD), Shunt (ARDS/pneumonia), Dead space (PE), Drive (opioid OD/neuromuscular).
- A2. The extra O₂ never reaches the blood passing through unventilated alveoli — it's bypassed. Needs PEEP/recruitment.
- A3. Obstruction → ↑CO₂ (Type 2); Shunt → ↓O₂ refractory; Dead space → ↓O₂ ± ↓EtCO₂; Drive → ↑CO₂.
- A4. Type 1 = oxygenation (low O₂; e.g., ARDS/pneumonia/PE); Type 2 = ventilation (high CO₂ → resp acidosis; e.g., opioid OD, COPD).

**B**
- B1. Bronchospasm + airway inflammation + mucus plugging.
- B2. **Silent chest** — no air movement, near-arrest; prepare for intubation.
- B3. **Rescue (bronchodilator) THEN steroid**; rinse mouth after steroid (thrush prevention).
- B4. Target ~88–92% in chronic retainers; **titrate**, don't withhold, don't blast — watch CO₂/drowsiness; get an ABG.
- B5. BiPAP first in COPD exacerbation (mortality benefit); intubation if BiPAP fails / patient exhausted / can't protect airway.

**C**
- C1. Shunt. Anticipate: lung-protective vent (Vt 6 mL/kg IBW + PEEP), prone positioning for severe, treat underlying cause (often sepsis).
- C2. Sudden dyspnea + sharp pleuritic pain + tachy + ↓O₂ + clear lungs; Wells → D-dimer (if low risk) → CTPA. Actions: O₂, upright, IV/monitor, anticoag pathway.
- C3. Once antibiotics are started, cultures become hard to interpret; identifying the bug guides narrowing therapy.
- C4. Tracheal deviation **away** from side, absent breath sounds, hypotension (± JVD) → **emergent needle decompression** → chest tube.
- C5. FiO₂, PEEP, Tidal volume; lung-protective Vt = **6 mL/kg ideal body weight** + PEEP, permissive hypercapnia.

**D**
- D1. **Position upright + O₂ to target + call/stay with patient** — don't leave to chart.
- D2. Hyperoxygenate before/after, ≤10–15 sec per pass, monitor SpO₂/HR; clean technique.
- D3. Albuterol = bronchodilator (rescue); ipratropium = anticholinergic bronchodilator (often combined in COPD); inhaled steroid = anti-inflammatory controller.
- D4. HOB ≥30°, daily oral care/chlorhexidine, daily sedation interruption + spontaneous breathing trial, DVT + ulcer prophylaxis.
- D5. Shunt physiology — more FiO₂ won't fix it; escalate (PEEP/vent/prone, treat cause).
