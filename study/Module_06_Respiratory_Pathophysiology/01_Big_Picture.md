# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **The lung fails in only four ways — obstruction, shunt, dead space, or loss of drive — and every common diagnosis maps to one or two of them.**

## The four failure modes (the whole module)
| Mode | What broke | Classic diseases | ABG pattern | Why O₂ helps (or doesn't) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Obstruction** | Airflow blocked / narrowed | Asthma, COPD | Type 2 (CO₂↑ → resp acidosis) | O₂ helps oxygenation; ventilation needs bronchodilators + work |
| **Shunt** | Alveoli **perfused but not ventilated** (flooded/collapsed) | ARDS, pneumonia, atelectasis, pulm. edema | Type 1 (hypoxemia refractory to O₂) | More FiO₂ won't fix it — need PEEP/recruitment |
| **Dead space** | Alveoli **ventilated but not perfused** | PE, very low CO | Hypoxemia ± low EtCO₂ | O₂ helps the perfused units; treat the cause (clot) |
| **Drive / pump** | Brain or muscles fail to ventilate | Opioid OD, neuromuscular dz, fatigue | Type 2 (CO₂↑) | Support ventilation (BiPAP/intubate); reverse cause |

## Engineering translation
| Physiology | Your language |
|---|---|
| Compliance | Stretchiness of lung+chest (ARDS = stiff; emphysema = floppy) |
| Resistance | Airway diameter — Poiseuille r⁴ (asthma/COPD narrow it) |
| PEEP | Positive end-expiratory pressure — keeps alveoli from collapsing on exhale |
| FiO₂ | Fraction of inspired O₂ (0.21 room air → 1.0 = 100%) |
| Tidal volume (Vt) | Air per breath; lung-protective ≈ 6 mL/kg ideal body weight |
| Lung-protective vent | Low Vt + PEEP to prevent volutrauma/barotrauma in ARDS |

## The spine
```
Lung must move air (ventilation) AND match it to blood (perfusion)
        ↓ one of 4 things breaks
[Obstruct] [Shunt] [Dead space] [Drive]
        ↓
ABG/SpO₂ tells you which → predictable nurse action
        ↓
Some get O₂ + bronchodilator; others get PEEP/vent; PE needs anticoag
```

## 5 anchors
1. **Shunt = hypoxemia O₂ won't fix.** (ARDS, pneumonia.)
2. **Obstruction usually = Type 2 failure** (CO₂↑) → respiratory acidosis (Mod 02).
3. **PE = dead space + sudden dyspnea** → anticoagulation pathway.
4. **Vent settings have 3 main knobs:** FiO₂, PEEP, Vt. Lung-protective Vt ≈ 6 mL/kg IBW.
5. **Airway/Breathing is always first.** Position upright + O₂ + call; *then* think.
