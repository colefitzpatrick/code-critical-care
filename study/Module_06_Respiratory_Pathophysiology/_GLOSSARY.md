## Failure modes
- **Obstruction** — narrowed/blocked airways (asthma, COPD).
- **Shunt** — alveoli perfused but NOT ventilated (flooded/collapsed): ARDS, pneumonia, atelectasis. Hypoxemia O₂ won't fix.
- **Dead space** — alveoli ventilated but NOT perfused: PE, very low CO. Wasted breaths.
- **Drive/pump failure** — brain/muscles fail to ventilate (opioid OD, neuromuscular). Type 2 failure.

## Diseases
- **Asthma** — reversible airway disease: bronchospasm + inflammation + mucus.
- **Status asthmaticus** — severe asthma not responding; silent chest = near arrest.
- **COPD** — emphysema + chronic bronchitis; air trapping; chronic CO₂ retention possible.
- **ARDS (Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome)** — diffuse alveolar damage → widespread shunt → refractory hypoxemia (Berlin criteria simplified: acute, bilateral, not cardiac, low PaO₂/FiO₂).
- **PE (pulmonary embolism)** — clot in pulmonary artery → dead space + sudden dyspnea.
- **DVT (deep vein thrombosis)** — leg/pelvic clot that can embolize to lungs.
- **Virchow's triad** — clot risks: stasis + hypercoagulability + endothelial injury.
- **Pneumonia** — alveolar infection (bacterial/viral/aspiration/fungal).
- **Atelectasis** — collapsed alveoli; prevented by deep breaths / IS.
- **Pneumothorax** — air in pleural space → lung collapse.
- **Tension pneumothorax** — emergency: air trapped, builds pressure, shifts trachea away → needle decompression.
- **Pulmonary edema** — fluid in alveoli (cardiogenic in HF; non-cardiogenic in ARDS).

## Ventilator vocab
- **FiO₂** — fraction of inspired oxygen (0.21 room air → 1.0 = 100%).
- **PEEP (Positive End-Expiratory Pressure)** — pressure that keeps alveoli open at end-expiration; recruits shunted lung.
- **Tidal volume (Vt)** — air delivered per breath.
- **Lung-protective ventilation (ARDS)** — Vt ≈ **6 mL/kg ideal body weight** + PEEP titrated to oxygenation.
- **Permissive hypercapnia** — accepting higher PaCO₂ to avoid lung injury.
- **Peak airway pressure** — rising = obstruction/displaced tube/pneumothorax.
- **DOPE** — vent troubleshooting: Displaced tube, Obstruction, Pneumothorax, Equipment.
- **VAP (ventilator-associated pneumonia)** — pneumonia in intubated patient; prevention bundle: HOB ≥30°, oral care, sedation interruption, DVT/ulcer prophylaxis.

## Devices / interventions
- **CPAP** — continuous positive airway pressure (one level; OSA).
- **BiPAP** — bilevel positive airway pressure (insp + exp pressures; COPD exacerbation, acute pulmonary edema). Non-invasive ventilation.
- **Tracheostomy** — surgical airway; humidify, suction PRN.
- **Chest tube** — drains air/fluid from pleural space; tidaling normal; continuous bubbling = leak.
- **Incentive spirometer** — deep-breath device; ~10×/hr awake; prevents atelectasis.
- **Pursed-lip breathing** — slows exhale; prevents airway collapse in COPD.
- **Prone positioning** — face-down for severe ARDS; improves V/Q + recruits posterior alveoli.

## Drug terms (quick)
- **β₂ agonist** — bronchodilator (albuterol).
- **Anticholinergic inhaler** — ipratropium; bronchodilator via different mechanism.
- **Inhaled steroid** — anti-inflammatory controller; rinse mouth after.
- **Mucolytic** — thins secretions (acetylcysteine).
- **Naloxone** — reverses opioid-induced respiratory depression.
