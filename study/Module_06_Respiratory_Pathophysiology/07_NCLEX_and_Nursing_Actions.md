# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (grades + exam)

Respiratory is the **#1 NCLEX priority topic** — Airway/Breathing beats everything else. Do this twice.

## A. The priority rule
**A → B → C.** Acute respiratory distress = ACT (don't "assess more"), then call.
- New respiratory complaint / silent chest / one-word sentences / accessory muscles / paradoxical breathing / drowsy hypercapnic patient = top priority on any assignment.
- "Which patient first?" → the new or worsening respiratory one almost every time.

## B. Assessment priorities
- **Rate, rhythm, effort (work of breathing), accessory muscle use, tripod, retractions**.
- **SpO₂ + mental status** (drowsy = either CO₂ retention or hypoxia — both bad).
- **Breath sounds** all fields (wheeze/crackle/absent/diminished).
- **Color/perfusion** (cyanosis = late).
- **Trend > one value.** Falling RR after opioids is worse than a one-off SpO₂.
- **Capnography/EtCO₂** catches hypoventilation **before** SpO₂ falls.

## C. Oxygen devices (low → high) + when
| Device | Rough FiO₂ | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Nasal cannula 1–6 L | ~24–44% | Mild, stable, eating |
| Simple mask | ~40–60% | Moderate |
| Non-rebreather | ~60–95% | Emergency / severe hypoxia |
| High-flow NC | Up to 100% with heat/humidity | Failing or peri-intubation |
| **BiPAP / CPAP** | High | COPD exacerbation, acute pulmonary edema, OSA — *non-invasive vent* |
| Intubation + vent | Controlled | Failure / can't protect airway / GCS ≤8 |

> **COPD retainers:** titrate to ordered target (often ~88–92%) — **don't withhold needed O₂; don't over-oxygenate.**

## D. Drug → why → monitor
| Class / drug | Reverses / does | Teach / monitor | Classic exam trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Albuterol (β₂ agonist)** | Bronchodilation (rescue) | Tachy, tremor, hypoK; use BEFORE steroid | Tachycardia normal — don't withhold |
| **Ipratropium (anticholinergic)** | Bronchodilation (slower); combined w/ albuterol in COPD | Dry mouth | Caution narrow-angle glaucoma |
| **Inhaled steroid (controller)** | ↓ airway inflammation | Daily; **rinse mouth** after; not for acute attack | Used as rescue = wrong |
| **Oral/IV steroid** | Severe attack | Hyperglycemia, mood, immune; taper | Don't stop abruptly |
| **Magnesium IV (severe asthma)** | Smooth-muscle relaxant | Watch BP, reflexes | Adjunct, not first-line |
| **Antibiotics (pneumonia/exac.)** | Bug-specific | **Cultures BEFORE first dose** | Skipping cultures |
| **Anticoagulants (PE)** | Stop clot growth | Bleeding precautions, INR/aPTT/levels | NSAIDs interaction |
| **Naloxone** | Reverses opioid → restores drive | Short half-life — may need re-dosing | Patient may renarcotize |
| **Surfactant (neonatal)** | Reduces surface tension (NRDS) | NICU specific | Adult ARDS — different mechanism |

## E. Labs/monitors you act on
- **ABG** (Module 02 algorithm): pH, PaCO₂ (ventilation), PaO₂ (oxygenation), HCO₃⁻.
- **SpO₂** — Hb saturation only; misleading in anemia, CO poisoning, poor perfusion, nail polish.
- **EtCO₂/capnography** — ventilation + circulation; rising EtCO₂ in CPR = ROSC; falling EtCO₂ in shock.
- **D-dimer** (PE workup, sensitive not specific), **chest X-ray**, **CTPA**.
- **Peak airway pressures** on vent (rising = obstruction/displaced/pneumothorax — DOPE).

## F. Patient teaching (NCLEX "shows understanding")
- **Asthma action plan**: peak-flow zones (green/yellow/red), early treatment, when to ED. **Use spacer.** Rinse mouth after steroid.
- **COPD**: pursed-lip breathing (slows exhale, prevents airway collapse), energy conservation, vaccines (flu, pneumococcal, COVID), **smoking cessation** (#1 yield), home O₂ safety (no smoking near).
- **PE / anticoagulants**: bleeding precautions, soft toothbrush, no NSAIDs without asking, consistent vitamin K (warfarin), med-alert ID, follow-up labs.
- **Pneumonia recovery**: full antibiotic course, hydration, incentive spirometer, cough/deep-breathe, follow up.
- **Smoking cessation** = the single highest-yield teaching for nearly every respiratory patient.

## G. Safety & delegation
- **Unstable airway/breathing, titrating O₂ on the unstable, first assessment, teaching, ABG interpretation, vent management = RN**.
- **UAP can:** apply prescribed stable O₂, ambulate stable patients, vitals on stable patients, set up incentive spirometer (RN teaches/evaluates), reposition.
- **VAP prevention bundle (intubated):** HOB ≥30°, daily oral care, daily sedation interruption + SBT, DVT + ulcer prophylaxis.
- **Suctioning:** hyperoxygenate before/after, ≤10–15 sec per pass, watch SpO₂/HR, sterile/clean per policy.

## H. NCLEX test-taking on respiratory
- "First action" in distress = **act on airway/breathing** (position/O₂/call/stay), not "document" or "reassess later."
- Worsening RR + effort + mental status > a single SpO₂ number.
- Don't **withhold O₂** from a hypoxic COPD patient — titrate.
- **Refractory hypoxemia on high O₂** → escalate (shunt — needs PEEP/vent), don't keep cranking FiO₂.
- ECG/arrhythmia question + COPD on theophylline = think toxicity.
