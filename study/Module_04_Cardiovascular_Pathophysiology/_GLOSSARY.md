# _GLOSSARY — Module 04 (plain English)

Lookup, not a read-through. **term** — meaning — *(link)*.

- **Heart failure (HF)** — heart pumps too weakly (systolic) or fills poorly (diastolic) to meet demand.
- **Left-sided HF** — backup into the lungs → pulmonary congestion (dyspnea, crackles).
- **Right-sided HF** — backup into the body → edema, JVD, weight gain.
- **RAAS** — renin–angiotensin–aldosterone system; retains Na⁺/water + vasoconstricts (a slow compensation).
- **SNS / catecholamines** — sympathetic drive: ↑HR, ↑contractility (fast compensation).
- **ACS / MI** — acute coronary syndrome / myocardial infarction = heart-muscle death from blocked coronary flow.
- **Troponin** — blood marker released when myocardium is damaged (confirms MI).
- **BNP** — hormone released by stretched ventricles; ↑ in heart failure/volume overload.
- **Ischemia → injury → infarction** — progressive stages of O₂-starved myocardium.
- **Atrial fibrillation (AF)** — disorganized atrial rhythm → stasis (clot/stroke) + poor filling.
- **JVD (jugular venous distension)** — visible neck-vein fullness; sign of right-sided volume backup.
- **Orthopnea** — breathlessness lying flat (relieved sitting up); left HF clue.
- **LV hypertrophy** — thickened left ventricle from chronic high afterload (hypertension).
- **Cardiogenic shock** — shock from pump failure (e.g., big MI) — *not* a fluid problem.
- **Afterload / preload / contractility** — Module 03 terms (resistance to eject / filling stretch / pump strength).
- **Reperfusion / cath lab** — restoring coronary blood flow (PCI/thrombolytics) — urgent in MI.
- **Pulse deficit** — apical rate > radial rate (seen in AF — not every beat perfuses).
