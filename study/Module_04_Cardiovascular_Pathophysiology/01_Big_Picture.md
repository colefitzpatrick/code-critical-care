# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **Cardiovascular disease = the pump, the pipes, or the electrics fail — and the body's
> compensations then make it worse.**

## The 4 problems (everything maps here)
| Problem | One line | Module 03 term that broke |
|---|---|---|
| **Heart failure** | pump too weak to meet demand | contractility / preload handling |
| **ACS / MI** | coronary blood flow blocked → muscle dies | coronary perfusion (Mod 01 Ch1) |
| **Arrhythmias** | electrical timing fails | HR / filling (Mod 03 Ch3) |
| **Hypertension** | chronic high afterload damages everything | SVR / afterload |

## The unifying engineering idea
A failing pump triggers the **baroreflex + RAAS** (renin-angiotensin-aldosterone) to
add HR, fluid, and vasoconstriction. Those *raise preload & afterload* on a pump that
already can't cope → **the compensation becomes the disease** (Module 01 Chain 6:
"compensatory ≠ good"). Almost every cardiac drug works by *reversing* a compensation.

## 5 ideas to anchor
1. Left HF → lungs flood (pulmonary congestion). Right HF → body floods (edema, JVD).
2. MI = ischemia → injury → infarction; **troponin** confirms muscle death.
3. The compensations (fluid, vasoconstriction, tachycardia) are the treatment *targets*.
4. "Unload the pump": ↓preload (diuretics), ↓afterload (vasodilators/ACE), ±↑contractility.
5. Time = muscle: MI and unstable arrhythmias are *minutes-matter* emergencies.
