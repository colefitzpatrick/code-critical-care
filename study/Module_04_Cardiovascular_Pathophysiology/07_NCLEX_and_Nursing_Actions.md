# 07 · NCLEX & Nursing Actions ⭐ (the grades + exam file)

This is where ABSN exam points and NCLEX questions live. Do it twice. Format is built
the way questions are *asked*: priority, assessment, meds, labs, teaching, safety,
delegation.

## A. The priority rule (answers ~half of cardiac NCLEX)
**ABC + "unstable before stable" + "acute before chronic."** On a cardiac question:
1. Airway/Breathing problem (left HF pulmonary edema, low SpO₂) → first.
2. Circulation/perfusion (chest pain, hypotension, lethal rhythm) → next.
3. Then chronic/teaching items.
> "New chest pain" or "O₂ 86%" beats "chronic stable edema" every time.

## B. Assessment priorities by problem
- **HF:** daily weight (same scale/time), lung sounds, edema, JVD, SpO₂, I&O, orthopnea.
- **MI/ACS:** pain (OPQRST), ECG, troponin trend, vitals, rhythm.
- **AF:** apical–radial pulse (pulse deficit), rhythm, neuro checks (stroke), bleeding (if anticoagulated).
- **HTN:** correct-technique BP (both arms, right cuff), end-organ signs.

## C. Drug → why → monitor (the pharm-exam engine)
| Drug class | Reverses | Key teaching / monitor | Classic exam trap |
|---|---|---|---|
| Loop diuretic (furosemide) | ↑preload | daily weight, **K⁺ (↓)**, ototoxicity, hypotension | hypokalemia → digoxin toxicity |
| ACE inhibitor (-pril) | ↑afterload/RAAS | **K⁺ (↑)**, dry cough, first-dose hypotension, **no in pregnancy** | ACE + K⁺-sparing = hyperkalemia |
| ARB (-sartan) | RAAS | as ACE, less cough | — |
| Beta-blocker (-olol) | SNS, O₂ demand | HR/BP **hold if HR <60 or SBP low**; don't stop abruptly | masks hypoglycemia |
| Nitroglycerin | preload/coronary | headache, hypotension; **no with PDE-5 (sildenafil)** | not with erectile-dysfunction drugs |
| Digoxin | ↑contractility/rate | apical pulse 1 min, **hold <60**, narrow therapeutic, ↓K⁺ ↑toxicity | yellow halos, nausea = toxicity |
| Antiplatelet/anticoagulant | clot | bleeding precautions, adherence, labs (INR/aPTT) | hold for procedures |
| Statin | plaque | muscle pain (rhabdo), liver | report dark urine/muscle pain |

## D. Labs you act on
- **Troponin** ↑ = myocardial injury (MI). · **BNP** ↑ = heart failure/volume overload.
- **K⁺** — the cardiac electrolyte; diuretics ↓, ACE/ARB ↑; out of range → monitor + call (Mod 01/02).
- **Digoxin level** narrow; **INR/aPTT** for anticoagulation.

## E. Patient teaching (NCLEX loves this — "which statement shows understanding?")
- **HF:** daily morning weight; **call for >2–3 lb/day or 5 lb/week**, increasing
  dyspnea, orthopnea; low-sodium diet; take meds even when feeling well; activity pacing.
- **HTN:** "silent" — keep taking meds though asymptomatic; change positions slowly
  (orthostatic); home BP log.
- **Anticoagulant:** bleeding precautions, soft toothbrush, no NSAIDs without asking,
  consistent vitamin-K diet (warfarin), med-alert ID.
- **Nitroglycerin:** sit down, one tab q5min ×3, **call 911 if no relief after first/×3**
  (protocol varies), store dark/airtight, expect headache.

## F. Safety & delegation (high-yield NCLEX)
- Telemetry/unstable, new chest pain, titrated drips, first assessment, teaching =
  **RN only** (not delegated to UAP).
- UAP **can**: stable daily weights, I&O, vitals on stable patients, ambulating stable
  patients (report values to RN).
- Always: fall/orthostatic precautions, bleeding precautions, continuous monitor for
  chest pain/arrhythmia.

## G. NCLEX test-taking on cardiac items
- "Select first action" → ABC, then assess before intervene (unless emergency = act).
- "Needs more teaching" = pick the **wrong/unsafe** statement.
- Don't bolus fluids into a failing pump; don't give a beta-blocker to a bradycardic
  patient; don't give nitro with sildenafil; don't push K⁺.
