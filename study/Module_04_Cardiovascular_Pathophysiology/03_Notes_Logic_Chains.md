# 03 · Notes as Logic Chains

*Because X → therefore Y → so the nurse does Z.* Bold → `_GLOSSARY.md`.

## Chain 1 — Heart failure: the compensation trap
Pump weak → ↓CO → baroreflex/**RAAS**/SNS kick in → ↑HR, fluid retention, vasoconstriction
**→** that ↑preload & ↑afterload on an already-failing pump → blood backs up → **congestion**
**→ nurse:** daily weight (earliest sign — **report >2–3 lb/day or 5 lb/week**), assess
lung sounds/edema/JVD, semi-Fowler's position, O₂, give **diuretics** (↓preload) +
**ACE/ARB** (↓afterload, blunt RAAS) + **beta-blocker** (blunt SNS, long-term), low-Na⁺
diet, strict I&O. *Watch K⁺: diuretics drop it, ACE/ARB raise it (Mod 02 link).*

## Chain 2 — Left vs right failure (high-yield exam point)
**Left HF** → blood backs up *behind the left side* → into lungs → **pulmonary
congestion**: dyspnea, orthopnea, crackles, ↓SpO₂, pink frothy sputum
**Right HF** → backs up *behind the right side* → into the body → **systemic
congestion**: dependent/sacral edema, JVD, weight gain, hepatomegaly
**→ nurse:** Left = airway/oxygenation priority (position, O₂, diuretics). Right =
volume/edema focus (weights, skin, fluid/Na⁺ limits). Most chronic HF is both.

## Chain 3 — ACS / MI (this is Module 01 Chain 1, in the heart)
Coronary artery blocked → no flow → no O₂/ATP → Na⁺/K⁺ pump fails → myocytes injure
then **infarct** (die) → **troponin** released (confirms muscle death) → pump &
electrical instability
**→ nurse (time = muscle):** recognize chest pain/dyspnea/diaphoresis → **ECG within
10 min**, O₂ if hypoxic, **aspirin**, nitroglycerin, pain control, IV access, continuous
monitor, alert provider/activate **cath lab** (reperfusion). Serial troponins + ECGs.

## Chain 4 — Atrial fibrillation
Disorganized atrial electrics → irregular ventricular rate + blood **stasis** in atria
**→** two dangers: (1) clot forms → embolizes → **stroke**; (2) fast/irregular rate →
poor filling → ↓CO
**→ nurse:** monitor rhythm/rate, anticipate **rate control** (beta-blocker/CCB) and
**anticoagulation** (stroke prevention) — teach bleeding precautions/adherence.

## Chain 5 — Hypertension: the silent afterload disease
Chronic ↑SVR → heart pumps against high afterload for years → **LV hypertrophy** →
eventual HF; also damages vessels → MI, stroke, kidney, retina
**→ nurse:** accurate BP technique, med adherence is everything (asymptomatic →
patients stop meds), teach lifestyle (Na⁺, weight, activity), monitor end-organ labs.

## Chain 6 — Cardiogenic shock (links Mod 01/03)
Massive pump failure (big MI) → CO collapses → systemic hypoperfusion
**→** distinguishing it: this is the **broken-pump** shock — NOT a fluid-bolus problem
(fluids flood the failing pump → worse pulmonary edema)
**→ nurse:** support perfusion/contractility, reperfuse, careful monitoring; recognizing
"pump, not tank" changes everything (Mod 01 Ch6 FMEA).

## Chain 7 — Why cardiac drugs are "anti-compensation"
Almost every drug reverses one compensation: **diuretic** ↓preload · **ACE/ARB** ↓afterload
+ blocks RAAS remodeling · **beta-blocker** blunts SNS/O₂ demand · **nitrate** ↓preload/
dilates coronaries · **antiplatelet/anticoagulant** stops clot · **statin** stabilizes plaque
**→ nurse:** if you know the compensation, you can predict the drug, its effect, and what
to monitor — that's how you answer pharm exam questions without memorizing lists.

---
### Compression (20 s)
> Pump fails → body compensates (HR/fluid/vasoconstriction) → compensation overloads
> the pump → congestion. Left=lungs, Right=body. MI = coronary Chain 1, troponin
> confirms, reperfuse fast. Treatment & every drug = reverse a compensation.
