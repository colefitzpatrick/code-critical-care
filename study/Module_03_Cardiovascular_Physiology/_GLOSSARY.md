# _GLOSSARY — Module 03 (plain English)

Lookup, not a read-through. **term** — meaning — *(engineering link)*.

- **Cardiac output (CO)** — blood pumped per minute = HR × SV.
- **Stroke volume (SV)** — blood ejected per beat. Set by preload, contractility, afterload.
- **Preload** — ventricular stretch/filling just before contraction. *(Input volume.)*
- **Afterload** — the pressure/resistance the ventricle must overcome to eject. *(Downstream R.)*
- **Contractility** — intrinsic pump strength, independent of load. *(Pump gain.)*
- **Frank–Starling** — more preload (stretch) → stronger contraction → bigger SV, up to a limit.
- **MAP (mean arterial pressure)** — average organ-perfusion pressure ≈ CO × SVR. Target often ≥ 65.
- **SVR (systemic vascular resistance)** — total resistance of the vessels. *(The R in ΔP=QR.)*
- **Diastole / systole** — heart filling / heart contracting. Coronary flow ≈ diastolic.
- **Baroreceptors / baroreflex** — pressure sensors + the fast loop defending BP (↑HR, ↑SVR).
- **Ejection fraction (EF)** — SV ÷ end-diastolic volume; % of filled volume ejected. Low = systolic failure.
- **Pulse pressure** — systolic minus diastolic; narrows in early hypovolemia.
- **Inotrope** — drug that changes contractility (positive = stronger).
- **Vasopressor** — drug that raises SVR (constricts vessels) → raises MAP.
- **Vasodilator** — drug that lowers afterload/SVR (eases ejection).
- **Poiseuille** — resistance ∝ 1/radius⁴; tiny caliber change = big resistance change.
