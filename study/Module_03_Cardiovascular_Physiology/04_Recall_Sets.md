<details><summary>ANSWER KEY</summary>

**A**
- A1. CO = HR × SV; MAP = CO × SVR.
- A2. Preload = ventricular stretch/filling before contraction; Afterload = resistance/pressure to eject against; Contractility = pump strength independent of load.
- A3. More fiber stretch (preload) → stronger contraction → larger SV (until overstretched).
- A4. Preload, contractility, afterload.
- A5. Resistance ∝ 1/r⁴ (Poiseuille) — small radius change → large resistance change.

**B**
- B1. Short diastole → less filling → ↓preload → ↓SV; CO = HR×SV can fall despite high rate.
- B2. Mostly in diastole (vessels squeezed in systole); fast HR/low diastolic pressure starves the myocardium → ischemia.
- B3. ↑HR, ↑SVR (vasoconstriction), ↑contractility. Trap: early shock can look "normal BP" with tachycardia — compensation hides it.
- B4. EF = SV/EDV (fraction ejected); low EF = systolic pump failure.

**C**
- C1. Fluids → preload; inotrope → contractility; vasodilator → afterload (↓); vasopressor → SVR (↑).
- C2. Vasodilation → SVR↓ → MAP = CO×SVR falls; fluids (preload) + norepinephrine (SVR) → MAP ≥ 65.
- C3. Falling BP means filling/SV is collapsing — rate control/cardioversion; "fast" ≠ good output.
</details>
