# 01 · The Big Picture

## One-sentence model
> **The heart is a demand-driven pump pushing blood through adjustable-resistance pipes;
> blood pressure is just flow × resistance.**

## Engineering translation (home turf)
| Physiology | Your language |
|---|---|
| MAP = CO × SVR | Ohm: ΔP = Q × R |
| Vessel resistance ∝ 1/r⁴ | Poiseuille — tiny radius change, huge R change |
| Preload | Filling/stretch before ejection (input volume) |
| Afterload | Pressure load the pump ejects against (downstream R) |
| Contractility | Pump "gain" independent of load |
| Frank–Starling | More stretch → stronger stroke (until overstretched) |
| Baroreflex | The BP control loop (Module 01 Chain 6) |

## The spine
```
Venous return → PRELOAD (stretch)
        ↓ Frank–Starling
Stroke Volume (SV) ← also set by CONTRACTILITY and AFTERLOAD
        ↓
CO = HR × SV
        ↓
MAP = CO × SVR  ───── sensed by BAROREFLEX → adjusts HR & SVR (neg. feedback)
        ↓
Organs perfused (incl. the heart's own muscle — in DIASTOLE)
        ↓ break any term
predictable BP change → predictable nurse action
```

## 5 ideas everything links to
1. **CO = HR × SV.** Two levers — and they can fight each other (too-fast HR ↓ SV).
2. **MAP = CO × SVR.** Every BP problem is a CO problem or an SVR problem (or both).
3. **SV has 3 dials:** preload, afterload, contractility.
4. **Frank–Starling:** the heart pumps out what it gets — until it's overstretched (→ HF).
5. **Coronary flow is diastolic.** Fast HR / low diastolic pressure starves the heart itself.
